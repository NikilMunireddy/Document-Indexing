'''Agaricomycetes''' is a [[class (biology)|class]] of [[fungi]]. The [[taxon]] is roughly identical to that defined for the '''Homobasidiomycetes'''  (alternatively called holobasidiomycetes) by Hibbett & Thorn,<ref name=Hibbett2001/> with the inclusion of [[Auriculariales]] and [[Sebacinales]]. It includes not only [[mushroom]]-forming fungi, but also most species placed in the deprecated taxa [[Gasteromycetes]] and Homobasidiomycetes.<ref name=Hibbett2007/>  Within the subdivision [[Agaricomycotina]], which already excludes the [[smut (fungus)|smut]] and [[rust (fungus)|rust]] fungi, the Agaricomycetes can be further defined by the exclusion of the classes [[Tremellomycetes]] and [[Dacrymycetes]], which are generally considered to be [[jelly fungus|jelly fungi]]. However, a few former "jelly fungi", such as ''[[Auricularia]]'', are classified in the Agaricomycetes.  According to a 2008 estimate, Agaricomycetes include 17 [[order (biology)|orders]], 100 [[family (biology)|families]], 1147 [[genus|genera]], and about 21000 [[species]].<ref name=Kirk2008/> Modern [[molecular phylogenetic]] analyses have been since used to help define several new orders in the Agaricomycetes: [[Amylocorticiales]], [[Jaapiales]],<ref name="Binder 2010"/> [[Stereopsidales]],<ref name="Sjökvist 2014"/> and [[Lepidostromatales]].<ref name="Hodkinson 2014"/>

==Classification==

Although [[morphology (biology)|morphology]] of the mushroom or [[basidiocarp|fruit body]] (basidiocarp) was the basis of early [[classification (biology)|classification]] of the Agaricomycetes,<ref name=Fries1874/> this is no longer the case.  As an example, the distinction between the ''[[Gasteromycetes]]'' (including [[puffball]]s) and ''Agaricomycetes'' (most other [[agaric]] mushrooms) is no longer recognized as a natural one—various puffball species have apparently evolved independently from agaricomycete fungi. However, most mushroom guide books still group the puffballs or gasteroid forms separate from other mushrooms because the older [[Elias Magnus Fries|Friesian]] classification is still convenient for categorizing fruit body forms.  Similarly, modern classifications divide the gasteroid order [[Lycoperdales]] between [[Agaricales]] and [[Phallales]].

==Features==

All members of the class produce basidiocarps and these range in size from tiny cups a few millimeters across to a giant [[polypore]] (''[[Phellinus ellipsoideus]]'') greater than several meters across and weighing up to {{convert|500|kg}}.<ref name=Dai2011/> The group also includes what are arguably the largest and oldest individual organisms on earth: the [[mycelium]] of one individual ''[[Armillaria gallica]]'' has been estimated to extend over 150,000&nbsp;square metres (37&nbsp;acres) with a mass of 10,000&nbsp;kg (22,000&nbsp;lb) and an age of 1,500&nbsp;years.<ref name=Smith1992/>

==Ecology==
Nearly all species are terrestrial (a few are aquatic), occurring in a wide range of environments where most function as decayers, especially of wood. However, some species are [[Forest pathology|pathogenic]] or [[parasitism|parasitic]], and yet others are [[symbiosis|symbiotic]] (i.e., [[Mutualism (biology)|mutualistic]]), these including the important [[ectomycorrhizal]] symbionts of forest trees. General discussions on the forms and [[biological life cycle|life cycles]] of these fungi are developed in the article on [[mushroom]]s, in the treatments of the various [[order (biology)|orders]] (links in table at right), and in individual species accounts.
