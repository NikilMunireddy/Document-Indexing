'''Her Majesty's Principal Secretary of State for the Home Department''' ('''Home Secretary''') is a senior official as one of the [[Great Offices of State]] within [[Government of the United Kingdom|Her Majesty's Government]] and head of the [[Home Office]]. The office is a [[Cabinet of the United Kingdom|British Cabinet]] level position. 

The Home Secretary is responsible for the [[Interior ministry|internal affairs]] of [[England and Wales]], and for [[immigration]] and [[citizenship]] for the United Kingdom. The remit of the Home Office also includes [[Law enforcement in the United Kingdom|policing]] in [[England and Wales]] and matters of [[national security]], as the Security Service, [[MI5]], is directly accountable to the Home Secretary. Formerly, the Home Secretary was the minister responsible for prisons and probation in England and Wales; however, in 2005 those responsibilities were transferred to the newly created [[Ministry of Justice (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Justice]] under the [[Lord Chancellor]].

The current Home Secretary is The Rt Hon. [[Theresa May]], MP, appointed on 12 May 2010 by Queen Elizabeth II on the advice of the [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]], [[David Cameron]], to serve in the [[First Cameron ministry|Conservative-Liberal Democratic coalition government]]. May was reappointed by Cameron on 8 May 2015 to serve as Home Secretary in the [[Second Cameron ministry|Conservative government]].
