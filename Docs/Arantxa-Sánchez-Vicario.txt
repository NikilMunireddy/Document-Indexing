'''Aránzazu Isabel María "Arantxa" Sánchez Vicario'''<ref>Vicario is not her married name. It is her mother's maiden name. In the [[Spanish naming customs|Spanish naming system]], every person has two surnames: the first one comes from the father, the second from the mother. A woman never changes surnames, regardless of whether she marries or divorces.</ref> ({{IPA-es|aˈɾantʃa ˈsantʃeθ biˈkaɾjo}}; born 18 December 1971) is a Spanish former World No. 1 professional [[tennis]] player. She won four [[Grand Slam (tennis)|Grand Slam]] singles titles, six Grand Slam women's doubles titles, and four Grand Slam mixed doubles titles. In 1994 she was crowned the ITF World Champion for the year.

==Career==
Arantxa Sánchez Vicario started playing tennis at the age of four, when she followed her older brothers [[Emilio Sánchez]] and [[Javier Sánchez]] (both of whom became professional players) to the court and hit balls against the wall with her first racquet. As a 17-year-old, she became the youngest winner of the women's singles title at the 1989 French Open, defeating World No. 1 [[Steffi Graf]] in the final. ([[Monica Seles]] broke the record the following year when she won the title at age 16.)

Sánchez Vicario quickly developed a reputation on the tour for her tenacity and refusal to concede a point. Commentator [[Bud Collins]] described her as "unceasing in determined pursuit of tennis balls, none seeming too distant to be retrieved in some manner and returned again and again to demoralize opponents" and nicknamed her the "Barcelona Bumblebee".<ref>{{cite book  | first=Bud | last=Collins | author2=Xander Hollander | year=1996 | title=Bud Collins' Tennis Encyclopedia | pages=434 | publisher=Visible Ink Press | isbn=1-57859-000-0}}</ref>

She won six women's doubles Grand Slam titles, including the US Open in 1993 (with [[Helena Suková]]) and [[Wimbledon Championships|Wimbledon]] in 1995 (with [[Jana Novotná]]). She also won four Grand Slam mixed doubles titles. In 1991, she helped Spain win its first-ever [[Fed Cup]] title, and helped Spain win the Fed Cup in 1993, 1994, 1995, and 1998. Sanchez Vicario holds the records for the most matches won by a player in Fed Cup competition (72) and for most ties played (58). She was ITF world champion in 1994 in singles.<ref>[http://www.sonyericssonwtatour.com/2/players/playerprofiles/PlayerBio2.asp?PlayerID=190008 Aranxta Sanchez Vicario WTA Bio Page]</ref>

Sánchez Vicario was also a member of the Spanish teams that won the [[Hopman Cup]] in 1990 and 2002.

Over the course of her career, Sánchez Vicario won 29 singles titles and 69 doubles titles before retiring in November 2002.<ref>{{cite news |title=TENNIS; Sánchez-Vicario Ends Her Career|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2002/11/13/sports/plus-tennis-sanchez-vicario-ends-her-career.html|accessdate=12 May 2009|date=13 November 2002 |publisher=[[The New York Times]]}}</ref> She came out of retirement in 2004 to play doubles in a few select tournaments as well as the [[2004 Summer Olympics]], where she became the only tennis player to play in five [[Olympics]] in the [[Olympics Games|Games]] history.<ref>{{cite news|title=Notebook: Rower makes history|url=http://www.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/2004-07-01-notebook-rowing_x.htm|accessdate=12 May 2009 |date=1 July 2004 |publisher=[[USA TODAY]]}}</ref> Sanchez Vicario is the most decorated Olympian in Spanish history with four medals – two silver and two bronze.<ref>{{cite web|title=Arantxa sanchez Vicario |url=http://www.tennisfame.com/famer.aspx?pgID=867&hof_id=244 |accessdate=12 May 2009 |publisher=[[International Tennis Hall of Fame]] |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/20070927183742/http://www.tennisfame.com/famer.aspx?pgID=867&hof_id=244 |archivedate=27 September 2007 }}</ref>

In 2005, ''TENNIS ''Magazine put her in 27th place in its list of [[TENNIS Magazine's 40 Greatest Players of the TENNIS Era|40 Greatest Players of the ''TENNIS'' era]] and in 2007, she was inducted into the [[International Tennis Hall of Fame]]. She was only the third Spanish player (and the first Spanish woman) to be inducted.

In 2009, Sánchez Vicario was present at the opening ceremony of [[Madrid]]'s [[Caja Mágica]], the new venue for the [[Madrid Open (tennis)|Madrid Masters]]. The second show court is named Court Arantxa Sánchez Vicario in her honour.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2009/05/09/sports/tennis/09tennis.html|title=Tennis Stadium Opens (and Closes) in Madrid | work=The New York Times | first=Christopher | last=Clarey | date=9 May 2009 | accessdate=25 April 2010}}</ref>

==Personal life==
She has been married twice: her first marriage to the sportswriter Juan Vehils ended in 2001. She married the businessman Josep Santacana in September 2008.<ref>[http://www.tennis.com/backcourt/general/backcourt.aspx?id=146044 Wedding Bells: Sanchez-Vicario Gets Married!] Tennis.com, 21 September 2008</ref> Their first baby, a girl also named Arantxa, was born on 27 February 2009.<ref>[http://www.zimbio.com/Arantxa+Sanchez-vicario/articles/5/Arantxa+Sanchez+Vicario+gave+birth+baby+girl Arantxa Sanchez Vicario gave birth to a baby girl] Zimbio.com, 3 March 2009</ref> Sánchez Vicario gave birth to their second child, a boy named Leo, on 28 October 2011.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.elperiodico.com/es/noticias/gente/arantxa-sanchez-vicario-alumbra-segundo-hijo-1203900|title=Arantxa Sánchez Vicario alumbra a su segundo hijo | work=[[El Periódico de Catalunya]]| date=3 November 2011 | accessdate=8 November 2011}}</ref>

In 2012 it was reported that, despite having earned $60 million over the course of her career, Sánchez Vicario's parents had "lost" all of her money, and that she was actually in debt to Spanish tax authorities.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sanchez Vicario says $60M in career earnings gone|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/story/2012-02-08/arantxa-sanchez-vicario-blames-parents-for-lost-money/53007004/1|publisher=''[[USA Today]]''|accessdate=3 July 2015|date=8 February 2012}}</ref>
