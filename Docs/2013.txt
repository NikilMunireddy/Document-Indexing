==Events==
===January===
* [[January 11]] &ndash; The French military begins a five-month intervention into the [[Northern Mali conflict]], targeting the militant [[Islamist]] [[Ansar Dine]] group.<ref>{{cite news|title=France launches Mali military intervention|url=http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/01/2013111135659836345.html|accessdate=January 11, 2013|agency=[[Al Jazeera]]|date=January 11, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-22664484|title=France army in key Mali withdrawal|publisher=[[BBC]]|date=May 25, 2013|accessdate=July 25, 2013}}</ref>
* [[January 16]]&ndash;[[January 20|20]] &ndash; Thirty-nine international workers and one security guard die in a [[In Amenas hostage crisis|hostage crisis]] at a [[natural gas]] facility near [[In Aménas]], [[Algeria]].<ref name=reuters1>{{cite news|title=Thirty hostages reported killed in Algeria assault|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2013/01/17/us-sahara-crisis-idUSBRE90F1JJ20130117|accessdate=January 17, 2013|agency=[[Reuters]]|date=January 17, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-21042659|title=Foreigners held hostage by terrorists in Algeria|publisher=BBC|date=January 16, 2013|accessdate=January 16, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.scotsman.com/news/international/algeria-hostage-crisis-briton-confirmed-dead-as-workers-held-hostage-by-terrorists-1-2740491|title=Algeria hostage crisis: Briton confirmed dead as workers held hostage by terrorists|work=[[The Scotsman]]|date=January 17, 2013|accessdate=July 11, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.cnn.com/2013/01/16/world/africa/algeria-attack/index.html|title=Islamists take foreign hostages in attack on Algerian oil field|publisher=[[CNN]]|date=January 16, 2013|accessdate=January 16, 2013}}</ref>

===February===
* [[February 12]] &ndash; [[North Korea]] conducts its [[2013 North Korean nuclear test|third underground nuclear test]], prompting [[International reactions to the 2013 North Korean nuclear test|widespread condemnation]] and tightened economic sanctions from the international community.<ref>{{cite news |last=Bilby |first=Ethan |url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2013/02/18/us-korea-north-eu-idUSBRE91H0E120130218 |title=EU approves tighter sanctions on North Korea |publisher=Reuters |date=February 18, 2013 |accessdate=July 11, 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-asia-21704862 |title=UN adopts tough new North Korea sanctions after nuclear test |date=March 7, 2013 |publisher=BBC |accessdate=March 16, 2013}}</ref>
* [[February 15]] &ndash; A [[Chelyabinsk meteor|meteor]] explodes over the Russian city of [[Chelyabinsk]], injuring 1,491 people and damaging over 4,300 buildings. It is the most powerful meteor to strike Earth's atmosphere [[Tunguska event|in over a century]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-21471942|title=Russia meteor eyewitness: 'Something like the sun fell'|publisher=BBC|date=February 15, 2013|accessdate= July 11, 2013}}</ref> The incident, along with a coincidental [[2012 DA14|flyby of a larger asteroid]], prompts international concern regarding the vulnerability of the planet to meteor strikes.<ref>{{cite news|last=McKie|first=Robin|title=Scientists unveil new detectors in race to save Earth from next asteroid|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/science/2013/feb/16/scientists-earth-asteroid|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|date=February 16, 2013|accessdate=July 11, 2013|location=London}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2013/02/17/science/space/dismissed-as-doomsayers-advocates-for-meteor-detection-feel-vindicated.html?_r=0|work=[[New York Times]]|title=Vindication for Entrepreneurs Watching Sky: Yes, It Can Fall|accessdate=April 22, 2013|date=February 16, 2013|first=William J.|last=Broad}}</ref>
* [[February 21]] &ndash; American scientists use a [[3D printer]] to create a living lab-grown [[ear]] from [[collagen]] and animal ear cell cultures. In the future, it is hoped, similar ears could be grown to order as [[organ transplant|transplant]]s for human patients suffering from ear trauma or amputation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/health/healthnews/9883441/Scientists-create-artificial-ear-using-3D-printing-and-living-cell-gels.html|title=Scientists create artificial ear using 3D printing and living-cell gels|work=[[Daily Telegraph]]|date=February 21, 2013|accessdate=February 25, 2013|location=London}}</ref>
* [[February 28]] &ndash; [[Pope Benedict XVI|Benedict XVI]] [[Resignation of Pope Benedict XVI|resigns]] as [[pope]], becoming the first to [[papal resignation|do so]] since [[Pope Gregory XII|Gregory XII]] in [[1415]], and the first to do so voluntarily since [[Pope Celestine V|Celestine V]] in [[1294]].<ref>{{cite news| url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/2013/02/28/uk-pope-resignation-idUKBRE91Q0BE20130228 |title=Benedict's reign ends with a promise to obey next pope |date=February 28, 2013 |author=Pullella, Philip |publisher=Reuters |accessdate=February 28, 2013}}</ref>

===March===

* [[March 24]] &ndash; [[Central African Republic conflict (2012–present)|2012–2013 Central African Republic conflict]]: [[Central African Republic]] President [[François Bozizé]] flees to the [[Democratic Republic of the Congo]], after rebel forces [[Central African Republic conflict (2012–present)#Fall of Bangui|capture]] the nation's capital, [[Bangui]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.rfi.fr/afrique/5min/20130324-rca-suivez-evenements-republique-centrafricaine-bozize-seleka|title=Centrafrique: Michel Djotodia déclare être le nouveau président de la république centrafricaine|accessdate=March 24, 2013|date=March 24, 2013|publisher=[[Radio France International]]|language=French}}</ref><ref name=BBC24march>{{cite news|title=Central African Republic: Rebels 'take palace as Bozize flees'|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-21915901|accessdate=March 24, 2013|publisher=BBC|date=March 24, 2013}}</ref><ref name="AJE CAR">{{Cite news | title = CAR rebels 'seize' presidential palace | publisher = Al Jazeera English | accessdate = March 24, 2013 | date = March 24, 2013 | url = http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2013/03/201332481729584103.html }}</ref>
* [[March 25]] &ndash; The European Union [[2012–13 Cypriot financial crisis|agrees to a €10&nbsp;billion economic bailout]] for [[Cyprus]]. The bailout loan will be equally split between the [[European Financial Stabilisation Mechanism]], the [[European Financial Stability Facility]], and the [[International Monetary Fund]]. The deal precipitates a [[2012–2013 Cypriot financial crisis|banking crisis]] in the island nation.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-21797888 | title=Eurozone and IMF agree 10bn-euro Cyprus bailout deal | date=March 17, 2013 | agency=BBC | accessdate=March 17, 2013 | work=BBC News}}</ref><ref name="Eurogroup bailout package agreement on 25 March">{{cite web|url=http://www.consilium.europa.eu/uedocs/cms_Data/docs/pressdata/en/ecofin/136487.pdf|title=Eurogroup Statement on Cyprus |format=PDF|publisher=[[Eurogroup]]|date=March 25, 2013|accessdate=March 25, 2013}}</ref>
* [[March 27]] &ndash; Canada becomes the first country to withdraw from the [[United Nations Convention to Combat Desertification]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ctvnews.ca/politics/harper-government-quietly-leaving-un-droughts-and-deserts-convention-1.1214065 |title=Canada only UN member to pull out of droughts and deserts convention|publisher=CTV News|accessdate=April 22, 2013|date=March 27, 2013}}</ref>
