'''''Time Out''''' is a magazine published by Time Out Digital Ltd.<ref name=gb>{{cite web| url=http://www.growthbusiness.co.uk/news-and-market-deals/mergers-and-acquisitions/1301613/oakley-capital-buys-stake-in-time-out.thtml| title=Oakley Capital buys stake in Time Out | date= 25 November 2010| publisher= Growth Business | accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref> Created in 1968, the [[London]]-based publication has expanded its editorial recommendations to 107 cities worldwide, across 39 countries, with a monthly audience of 40 million readers across content distribution platforms including mobile, website, magazine and events.<ref name=gor>{{cite web| url=http://gorkana.com/news/consumer/little-black-book/gorkana-meetstime-out-london/| title=Gorkana meets...Time Out London | date= 26 January 2015| publisher= Gorkana| accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref> In 2012, the magazine became a free publication with a weekly readership of over 307,000. In addition to print, the ''Time Out London'' website has seven million unique users and one million page views per day.<ref name = gor/> ''Time Out’s'' global market presence includes partnerships with [[Nokia]] and [[mobile apps]] for [[iOS]] and [[Android (operating system)|Android]] operating systems.<ref name=pe>{{cite web| url=https://www.pehub.com/2011/12/time-out-acquires-kelkoo-select/| title=Time Out acquires Kelkoo Select| date= 12 December 2011| publisher= PE Hub| accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref> It was the recipient of the International Consumer Magazine of the Year award in both 2010 and 2011 and the renamed International Consumer Media Brand of the Year in 2013 and 2014.<ref name=sport>{{cite web| url=http://sports.tmcnet.com//news/2012/01/23/6070746.htm| title=Time Out acquires Whatsonstage.com| date= 23 January 2012| publisher= Sports Techy| accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref><ref name=to>{{cite web| url=http://www.timeout.com/about/time-out-group/latest-news/worldwide-brand-of-the-year-again| title=Worldwide brand of the year… again!| date= 15 July 2014| publisher= Time Out| accessdate=15 June 2015}}</ref>

==History==
''Time Out'' started as a magazine created in 1968 by [[Tony Elliott (publisher)|Tony Elliott]] who used birthday money to produce a one-sheet pamphlet.<ref name=ft>{{cite web| url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/419e3992-0665-11e2-bd29-00144feabdc0.html#axzz3YjFzkthP| title=Time Out abandons its cover price| author = Robert Budden| date= 24 September 2012| publisher= Financial Times| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> The first product was titled "Where It's At," before being inspired by [[Dave Brubeck]]’s album ''[[Time Out (album)|Time Out]]''.<ref name=refb>{{cite web| url=http://www.referenceforbusiness.com/history2/57/Time-Out-Group-Ltd.html| title=Time Out Group Ltd. | publisher= Reference for business| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> The magazine was initially a counter-culture publication which took a non-conformist stance on issues such as gay rights, racial equality, and police harassment.<ref name=camp>{{cite web| url=http://www.campaignlive.co.uk/news/1213088/| title= Time Out makes capital gains as free magazine| date= 26 September 2013| publisher= Campaign| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref><ref name=adweek>{{cite web| url=http://www.adweek.com/news/press/can-time-out-s-tony-elliott-weather-storm-132910?page=1| title= Can Time Out's Tony Elliott weather the storm? The founder of iconic magazines cedes control to keep his dream alive| author = Lucia Moses| date= 27 June 2011| publisher= Adweek| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> As one example of its early editorial stance, in 1976 London's ''Time Out'' published the names of 60 purported CIA agents stationed in England.<ref name="'70s 51">{{cite book |title= How We Got Here: The '70s|last= Frum|first= David|authorlink= David Frum|coauthors= |year= 2000|publisher= Basic Books|location= New York, New York|isbn= 0-465-04195-7|page= 51}}</ref> Early issues had a print run of around 5,000 and would evolve to a weekly circulation of 110,000 as it shed its radical roots.<ref name = adweek/><ref>http://www.marketingweek.co.uk/item/58896/259/263/3 Reynolds, John, "Moving Beyond its City Limits", ''Marketing Week'', 6 December 2007.</ref>

Elliott launched ''Time Out New York'' (''TONY''), his North American magazine debut, in 1995. The magazine procured young and upcoming talent to provide cultural reviews for young New Yorkers at the time.<ref name = adweek/> The success of ''TONY'' led to the introduction of ''Time Out New York Kids'', a quarterly magazine aimed at families. The expansion continued with Elliott licensing the Time Out brand worldwide spreading the magazine to 36 cities including Istanbul, Dubai, Beijing, Hong Kong and Lisbon.<ref name = adweek/>

Additional Time Out products included travel magazines, city guides, and books.<ref name = adweek/> In 2010, Time Out became the official publisher of travel guides and tourist books for the London 2012 Olympic and Paralympic Games.<ref name=book>{{cite web| url=http://www.thebookseller.com/news/carlton-wiley-and-time-out-official-publishers-london-olympics | title=Carlton, Wiley and Time Out official publishers for London Olympics| author = Catherine Neilan| date= 17 March 2010| publisher= The Bookseller| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> There are currently over 100 guide books available from Time Out through its publishing partner Ebury Publishing (an imprint of Penguin Random House).<ref name=rh>{{cite web| url=http://www.randomhouse.co.uk/about-us/about-us/companies/uk-companies-and-imprints/ebury-publishing/time-out| title=Time Out | publisher= The Random House Group| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref>

''Time Out''{{’}}s need to expand to digital platforms led to Elliott, sole owner of the group until November 2010, to sell half of ''Time Out'' London and 66 percent of ''TONY'' to private equity group Oakley Capital, valuing the company at £20million.<ref name=bbc20101126>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-11844925|title=Time Out publisher sells 50% stake|publisher=BBC News|date=26 November 2010|accessdate=26 November 2010}}</ref> The group is now owned by Tony Elliott and Oakley Capital, founded by [[Peter Dubens]].<ref name="ft"/><ref name="bbc20101126"/> The agreement provided capital for investment to expand the brand. ''Time Out'' has subsequently launched websites for an additional 33 cities including Delhi, Washington D.C., Boston, Manchester and Bristol.<ref name = adweek/>

===''Time Out London''===
The London edition of ''Time Out'' became a free magazines in September 2012.<ref name = camp/> ''Time Out's'' London magazine was hand distributed at central London stations, and received its first official ABC Certificate for October 2012 showing distribution of over 305,000 copies per week which was the largest distribution in the history of the brand.<ref name=guard>{{cite web|url= http://www.theguardian.com/media/2012/aug/02/time-out-free-london-edition| title=Time Out goes free, London edition of listings magazine to drop cover price| author = Mark Sweney| date= 1 August 2012| publisher= The Guardian | accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mediaweek.co.uk/news/1161516/Free-Time-hits-three-month-circulation-target-first-month|title=Free Time Out hits three-month circulation target in first month|work=mediaweek.co.uk}}</ref> This strategy increased revenue by 80 percent with continued upsurge.<ref name=folio>{{cite web| url=http://www.foliomag.com/2015/54876/| title=Time Out New York Transforms Into A (Mostly) Free Weekly| author = Caysey Welton | date= 2 April 2015| publisher= Folio| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> ''Time Out'' has also invited a number of guest columnists to write for the Magazine. The current columnist (2014) is Giles Coren.<ref name=media>{{cite web| url=http://www.mediaweek.co.uk/article/1320240/time-hires-giles-coren-columnist| title=Time Out hires Giles Coren as columnist| author = Arif Durrani| date= 11 March 2014| publisher= Media Week| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref>

===''Time Out New York''===
In April 2015, ''Time Out'' switched its New York magazine to the free distribution model to increase the reader base and grow brand awareness.<ref name = folio/> This transition doubled circulation by increasing its Web audience, estimated around 3.5 million unique visitors a month.<ref name=nypost>{{cite web| url=http://nypost.com/2015/04/01/time-out-new-york-switching-to-all-free-distribution-model| title=Time Out New York switching to all-free distribution model | author = Keith J. Kelly| date= 1 April 2015| publisher= New York Post| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref> ''Time Out'' increased its weekly magazine circulation to over 305,000 copies complementing millions of digital users of ''Time Out'' New York.<ref name = folio/><ref name=nypost/> Free magazines are distributed at bars, restaurants, gyms, subway stations, and theaters and the web content separate from the magazine is free. In addition, a subscription service is offered to those that prefer the magazine to be physically delivered and paid subscribers have access to a digital edition of the magazine.<ref name = folio/>

===''Time Out New York Kids''===
In 1996, following the launch of ''TONY'' magazine, ''Time Out New York Kids'' was launched as a paid-for quarterly title available via subscription and news stand with a circulation of 55,000.<ref name=to2>{{cite web| url=http://www.timeout.com/about/commercial-opportunities/north-america/new-york-city/time-out-new-york-kids| title=Time Out New York Kids| publisher= Time Out| accessdate=16 June 2015}}</ref>
