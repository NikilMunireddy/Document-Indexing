'''Győri ETO FC''' ({{IPA-hu|ˈɟøːri ˈeto}}), ''Győri Egyetértés Torna Osztály Football Club'' (literally: ''Concordance Gymnastics Department of Győr'') or just '''Győr''', is a [[Football (soccer)|football]] club from the city of [[Győr]] in [[Hungary]]. They are best known for reaching the semi-finals of the [[European Cup 1964-65]]. The club has won the [[NB1|Hungarian League]] four times. In 1904 the club was founded as ''Győri Vagongyár ETO'' and has undergone many name changes since. The colours of the club are green and white.

==History==

[[File:Jeny-rudolf.jpg|thumb|right|150px|[[Rudolf Jeny]] managing Győr in the [[1955 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1955]] season of the [[Hungarian League]]]]
Győr first reached the [[Nemzeti Bajnokság I|Hungarian First Division]] in 1937, however their stay was brief. They were relegated straight back to the second division after finishing in 13th out of 14 teams in the [[1937-38 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1937–38]] season. In 1946 the club again reached the first division and this time they played 10 consecutive seasons in the top flight, until in [[1955 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1955]] they were relegated again. They again spent one season in the first league in [[1958-59 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1958]]. After their next comeback in 1960, they played 55 years in the top flight, until they were relegated to the [[Nemzeti Bajnokság III]] at the end of the [[2014–15 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2014–15 season]].

===1960s===
In 1963 Győri ETO FC won the [[Hungarian National Championship I]] title for the first time.
As a consequence, the team could enter the [[European Cup 1964-65]] season. On 2 September 1964 Győr beat [[Chemie Leipzig]] 2–0 in [[Leipzig]]. In the second leg Győr won 4–2 and entered the first round of the qualification.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1964/matches/round=935/index.html|title=1964/65 European Champions Clubs' Cup|date=8 August 2011|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the first round Győr played their first match at home against [[Lokomotiv Sofia]] and they won the first leg by 5–3. In [[Sofia]] they lost to 4–3 but won on aggregate 8–7.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1964/matches/round=936/index.html|title=1964/65 European Champions Clubs' Cup|date=8 August 2011|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the quarter finals Győr played the [[Dutch people|Dutch]] [[Door Wilskracht Sterk]]. The first leg was played in [[Amsterdam]] at the [[Olympisch Stadion (Amsterdam)|Olympisch Stadion]] and the final result was 1–1. In the second leg at home Győr won the match 1–0, and therefore won 2–1 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1964/matches/round=937/index.html|title=1964/65 European Champions Clubs' Cup|date=8 August 2011|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the semi-finals Győr faced the [[Portuguese people|Portuguese]] giant club [[S.L. Benfica|Benfica]] and lost their first match 1–0 at home at the [[Népstadion]] (now [[Puskás Ferenc Stadium]]). In the second leg Benfica won 4–0 at the [[Estádio da Luz (1954)|Estádio da Luz]] which resulted in the departure of Győr from the European Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1964/matches/round=938/index.html|title=1964/65 European Champions Clubs' Cup|date=8 August 2011|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In 1965 Győr won the [[Hungarian Cup]] for the first time by beating [[Diósgyőri VTK]] 4–0 in the final held at the [[Népstadion]] therefore Győr could enter the [[UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966-67]] season. In the first round Győr lost 1–0 ([[Luciano Chiarugi|Chiarugi]]) to [[ACF Fiorentina]] in [[Florence]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=781/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Fiorentina 1–0 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> but in the second leg they could beat the Italian club by 4–2 (Stolcz 2, Varsányi, [[Árpád Orbán|Orbán]]) therefore they could advance on aggregate
.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=781/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Győr 4–2 Fiorentina|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the first leg of the second round Győr beat [[SC Braga]] 3–0 at home.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=782/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Győr 3–0 Braga|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> Although they lost 2–0 away, they could qualify for the quarter finals of the Cup Winners' Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=782/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Braga 2–0 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the quarter-finals Győr won their first match 2–1 against the [[Belgian people|Belgian]] [[Standard Liège]] at home<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=783/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Győr 2–1 Standard Liege|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> but they lost the second leg of the tie 2–0 which resulted the elimination of the club from the cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1966/round=783/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1966–67: Standard Liege 2–0 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref>

[[File:Nandor Hidegkuti.jpg|150px|right|thumb|[[Nándor Hidegkuti]] won the [[1963 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1963]] season with the club]]
In 1966 Győr could keep the trophy of the Hungarian Cup by beating [[Ferencvárosi TC]] in a [[two-legged tie]]. The first match ended in a 1–1 draw, while Győr could win away by 3–2 obtaining their second cup title in a row. In the [[UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1967-68]] Győr beat the [[Cyprus|Cypriot]] [[Apollon Limassol]] 5–0 at home,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1967/round=786/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1967–68: Győr 5–0 Apollon Limassol|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> while 4–0 away.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1967/round=786/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1967–68: Apollon Limassol 0–4 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the second round Győr faced with the [[Italian people|Italian]] giant club [[AC Milan]]. On 22 November 1967, at home both Győrfi and [[Angelo Sormani|Sormani]] scored two goals ending the match in a 2–2 draw.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1967/round=787/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1967–68: Győr 2–2 AC Milan|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the second leg, on 7 December 1967, at the [[Stadio Giuseppe Meazza]] Szaló scored in the 6th minute which was equalized by [[Pierino Prati|Prati]] in the 22nd minute which resulted a 1–1 draw eliminating Győr from the cup without a lost match against AC Milan.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1967/round=787/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1967–68: AC Milan 1–1 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref>

In 1967 Győr could triple their Hungarian Cup titles by beating [[Salgótarjáni BTC]] 1–0 in the final at the Népstadion in front of 3,000 spectators. In the [[UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1968-69]] Győr would have entered the first round of the tournament but due to the [[Warsaw Pact invasion of Czechoslovakia]] the club were forced to withdraw their participation.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1968/round=791/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1968–69: Participation withdrawn|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1968/intro.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1968–69: Slovan shine despite political clouds|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref>

===1970s===
In 1979 Győr won the Hungarian Cup for the fourth time by beating Ferencvárosi TC 1–0 at the Népstadion in front of 10,000 spectators. In the [[UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1979-80]] Győr faced the Italian giant club, [[Juventus F.C.]]. On 19 September 1979, Juventus beat Győr 2–0 in Turin. Pozsgai's own goal in the 64th minute was followed by [[Antonio Cabrini|Cabrini]]'s goal in the 73rd minute at the [[Stadio Olimpico di Torino]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1979/round=851/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1979–80: Juventus 2–0 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the second leg of the tie, on 3 October 1979, Győr was leading by 2–0 ([[Giuseppe Furino|Furino]]'s scored an own goal in the 4th minute and Póczik in the 23rd) but in the second half [[Franco Causio|Causio]] scored in the 52nd minute ending the match in 2–1 victory over Juventus which was not enough to advance in the cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://en.archive.uefa.com/competitions/ecwc/history/season=1979/round=851/index.html|title=UEFA Cup Winners' Cup 1979–80: Győr 2–1 Juventus|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref>

===1980s===
[[File:József Verebes (1987).jpg|thumb|left|200px|The longest serving and most successful manager, József Verebes a.k.a. "The magician" won the Hungarian League twice [[1981-82 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1982]] and [[1982-83 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1983]] with Győr]]
Győr reached their zenith in the early 1980s winning the [[1981-82 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1981–82]] and the [[1982-83 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1982–83]] season of the Hungarian League. [[Péter Hannich]] and [[Lázár Szentes]] scored 43 goals together out of the 102 goals during the 1981–82 season. The József Verebes led team gained 49 points applying the Dutch style of playing football. The following season Győr won the championship only in the last round gaining 44 points while Ferencváros came second with 43 points. In the 1982–83 season Győr scored 20 goals less than the previous season which was due to Lázár Szentes injuries while Péter Hannich scored 20 goals becoming the second top goal scorer in the season.
Since the team won the 1981–82 season they entered the [[European Cup 1982-83]]. In the first leg of the first round of the tournament on 15 September 1982, [[Standard Liège]] hosted Győr at the [[Stade Maurice Dufrasne]] in [[Liège]]. The Belgian club beat Győr by 5–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1982/matches/round=1034/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1982–83: Standard Liege 5–0 Győr|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref> In the second leg, on 29 September 1982, Győr beat 3–0 Standard Liege at home but it was not sufficient to qualify for the next round.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1982/matches/round=1034/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1982–83: Győr 3–0 Standard Liege|date=22 June 2013|publisher=uefa.com}}</ref>

In the [[European Cup 1983-84]] Győr beat the [[Icelandic people|Icelandic]] [[Knattspyrnufélagið Víkingur|Víkingur]] in the first leg of the first round. Győr won the first leg 2–1 with the goals of Magyar and Burcsa.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1983/matches/round=1/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1983–84: Győr 2–1 Vikingur|date=14 September 1983|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> They won also in Iceland by 2–0 with the goals of Magyar and Szentes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1983/matches/round=1/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1983–84: Vikingur 0–2 Győr|date=28 September 1983|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second round Győr had to face a much more difficult opponent the [[Soviet people|Soviet]] [[FC Dinamo Minsk|Dinamo Minsk]]. The first match was played in Győr and the Soviet club won it by 6–3.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1983/matches/round=2/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1983–84: Győr 3–6 Dinamo Minsk|date=19 October 1983|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the away match Dinamo won 3–1 and eliminated the Hungarian club from the European cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=1983/matches/round=2/index.html|title=UEFA European Cup 1983–84: Dinamo Minsk 3–1 Győr|date=2 November 1983|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In the [[1983-84 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1983–84]] season of the national league Győr finished [[Runner-up|runners-up]] in the Hungarian League therefore they qualified for the [[1984-85 UEFA Cup]]. On 19 September 1984, Győr played with [[Manchester United F.C.]] at the [[Old Trafford Stadium]] and the final result was 3–0 to the English side.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1984/matches/round=1121/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1984–85: Manchester United 3–0 Győr|date=19 September 1984|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> At home Győr drew with Manchester which resulted their farewell from the international cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1984/matches/round=1121/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1984–85: Győr 2–2 Manchester United|date=3 October 1984|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In the [[1984-85 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1984–85]] season of the Hungarian League Győr came second after [[Budapest Honvéd]]. On international level Győr entered [[1985-86 UEFA Cup]]. On 18 September 1985, Győr hosted the [[Czech people|Czech]] [[FC Bohemians 1905]] and beat 3–1. On 2 October 1985, the final result was 3–1 to the Prague team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1985/matches/round=1127/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1985–86: Győr 3–1 Bohemians 1905|date=18 September 1985|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the extra time the Czech team scored another goal and qualified for the next round.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1985/matches/round=1127/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1985–86: Bohemians 1905 4–1 Győr|date=2 October 1985|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In the [[1985-86 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1985–86]] season of the Hungarian League the club finished third therefore they could enter the [[1986-87 UEFA Cup]]. On 17 September 1986, Győr was hosted by [[FC Dinamo Minsk|Dinamo Minsk]] and Győr could win 4–2 away.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1986/matches/round=599/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1986–87: Dinamo Minsk 2–4 Győr|date=17 September 1986|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> On 1 October 1986, Győr lost 1–0 at home but could enter the second round on 4–3 aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1986/matches/round=599/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1986–87: Győr 0–1 Dinamo Minsk|date=1 October 1986|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second round, on 22 October 1986, Győr was beaten 4–0 by [[Torino F.C.]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1986/matches/round=600/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1986–87: Torino 4–0 Győr|date=22 October 1986|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> On 5 November 1986, Győr drew with Torino which resulted their farewell from the UEFA Cup.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=1986/matches/round=600/index.html|title=UEFA Cup 1986–87: Győr 1–1 Torino|date=5 November 1986|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In the [[1986-87 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1986–87]] season the club finished only 10th while in the following year they came closer to the top of the league by reaching fourth place.

===1990s===
[[File:Zoltan Varga 1973.jpg|thumb|right|225px|[[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]] legend, [[Zoltán Varga (footballer)|Zoltán Varga]] managed Győr in [[2000-01 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2001]] and [[2002-03 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2003]]]]
The 1990s did not start promising for the club since they finished only 11th in the [[1990-91 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1990–91]] and the [[1991-92 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1991–92]] season of the Hungarian League. In [[1995-96 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1995–96]] the club finished 15th in the league and had to play play-offs against the Western Hungarian rivals [[FC Sopron]]. The first match was won by Győr 2–0 while the away match was lost 2–1 which resulted in Győr remaining in the first division. After reaching the nadir of the history of the club, in the [[1996-97 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1996–97]] season Győr finished 9th and secured a place for the following season. In the [[1997-98 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1997–98]] and [[1998-99 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|1998–99]] seasons the club finished fourth which were considered the best positions in the 1990s.

===2000s===
In January 2000 Antonio A. Paes join was selected for a tryout
In January 2003 [[Aurél Csertői]] was appointed as the coach of the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=50206.html|title=Csertöi lands Györ post|date=17 January 2003|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>
[[Attila Pintér (footballer born 1966)|Attila Pintér]] became the coach of the Győri ETO FC in 2009. During his coaching the team finished third in the [[Hungarian National Championship I 2009–10|Hungarian League 2009–10]] season after beating [[Videoton FC]] in the last round.
On international turf the team entered the [[Europa League 2010-11]] season. In the first qualifying round Győr played their first match against [[FC Nitra]]. The final result was 2–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000124/match=2002474/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Nitra 2–2 Győr|date=1 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second leg Attila Pintér's team could win 3–1 at the [[ETO Park]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000124/match=2002499/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Győr 3–1 Nitra|date=8 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second qualifying round Győr played their first match in [[Kazakhstan]] against [[Atyrau]]. The match finished 2–0 to Győr thanks to the goals of [[Linas Pilibaitis]] and [[Fouad Bouguerra]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000125/match=2002529/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Atyrau 0–2 Győr|date=15 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second leg Győr could win 2–0. The goals were scored by [[Rati Aleksidze]] and [[Mihai Nicorec]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000125/match=2002569/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: UEFA Europa League: Győr 2–0 Atyrau|date=22 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the third qualifying round Győr played with the [[French people|French]] [[Montpellier HSC]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=1508123.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Montpellier itching to get going|date=27 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> The first match was played at the [[ETO Park]] and the final result was 1–0 to the French team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000126/match=2002775/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Győr 0–1 Montpellier|date=29 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> The only goal of the match was scored by [[Olivier Giroud]]. In the second leg Győr won 1–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000126/match=2002810/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Montpellier 0–1 Győr|date=5 August 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> The only goal was scored by [[Valentin Babić]]. In extra time there were no more goals therefore the teams had to decide the match in the penalty shootout. Győr won 4–3 on penalties and entered the play-offs of the Europa League. Győr played the [[Croatian people|Croatian]] [[Dinamo Zagreb]] in the play-off of the [[Europa League 2010-11]]. The first match was won by Zagreb 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000127/match=2003091/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Győr 0–2 Dinamo Zagreb|date=19 August 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second leg at the [[Stadion Maksimir]] győr lost to 2–1 and were eliminated from the Europa League on 4–1 aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2011/matches/round=2000127/match=2003128/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League: Dinamo Zagreb 2–1 Győr|date=26 August 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>
Due to the increase of the number of matches the team were unable to do well both in the Hungarian League and in the Europa League. The results in the domestic league were not satisfying for the management, therefore Attila Pintér and the management mutually terminated the contract between each other and the management appointed [[Aurél Csertői]] as the manager.

===2010s===
[[File:Pintér Attila edző.jpg|thumb|right|200px|[[Attila Pintér (footballer born 1966)|Attila Pintér]] managed to win the fourth [[Hungarian League]] title in the [[2012–13 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2012–13]] season]]
In 2011 [[Aurél Csertői]] was appointed as the manager of the team. Győr started the [[2011-12 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2011–12]] season with seven consecutive wins. On 19 June 2011 UEFA suspended the club for violations of licensing rules. As a consequence the club cannot participate in European competitions for three consecutive seasons.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.footballinfo.org/gyor-is-suspended-from-european-cup/|title="Gyor" is suspended from European cup|publisher=footballinfo.org|date=19 June 2011|accessdate=19 June 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Györ suspended and fined, MLSZ fined|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefa/footballfirst/matchorganisation/disciplinary/news/newsid=1644479.html|publisher=[[UEFA]].com|accessdate=12 April 2012|date=17 June 2011}}</ref> In March 2012 Aurél Csertői and the club agreed to his departure and Attila Pintér was re-appointed as the manager of the club. In the [[2011-12 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2011–12]] season of the Hungarian League Győr finished third, however due to the suspension of the UEFA they could not enter the [[Europa League]].

In the [[2012–13 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2012/13]] season of the Hungarian League Győr was leading the table 6 points clear.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1906911.html|title=Resurgent Győr rising to the challenge|date=18 December 2012|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

On 12 May 2013, Győr ended a 30-year wait for the Hungarian League title by beating [[Ferencvárosi TC]] at home by 1–0, while their rival [[Videoton FC]] lost to [[Debreceni VSC]] 2–1 in the 27th weekday of the championship. In the 2012–13 season they went on a 22-match unbeaten run before back-to-back defeats knocked them off their stride. But neither Videoton FC nor [[MTK Budapest FC]] could sustain the challenge therefore Győr could wrap up their fourth title with room to spare.
<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1950942.html|title=Gutsy Győr crowned Hungarian champions|date=12 May 2013|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

On 22 May 2013, Győr could have double their success by winning the [[2012–13 Magyar Kupa]] against [[Debreceni VSC]] at the [[Bozsik Stadion]], [[Budapest]]. Győr was winning by 1–0 thanks to the goal by [[Nemanja Andrić]] in the 19th minute but in the second half Debrecen's [[Adamo Coulibaly]] scored two goals which resulted Debrecen's victory over Győr in the Hungarian Cup final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1955271.html|title=Debreceni deny Győr in Hungarian Cup final|date=22 May 2013|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

On 17 July 2013, Győr hosted [[Maccabi Tel Aviv F.C.]] and lost to 2–0 in the second qualifying round of the [[UEFA Champions League 2013-14]] season.<ref>http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2014/matches/round=2000476/match=2011687/index.html</ref> In the second tie Győr also lost 2–1 which resulted the early farewell of the club.

On 19 December 2013, Pintér resigned from his position after he was appointed as the [[head coach]] of the [[Hungary national football team|Hungarian national football team]] by the head of the [[Hungarian Football Federation]], [[Sándor Csányi (banker)|Sándor Csányi]], in [[Telki]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuro/qualifiers/news/newsid=2039529.html|title=Hungary pick Pintér to replace Egervári|date=19 December 2013|publisher=www.uefa.com}}</ref>

==Crest and colours==
The colurs of the club are green and white. This combination of colours is very common in Hungary, as it is also used by clubs such as [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]], [[Szombathelyi Haladás]], [[Paksi SE|Paks]]. The crest of the club includes a cock sitting on a cross.

==Stadium==
{{Main|ETO Park}}
{{wide image|ETO Park view from hotel room.jpg|1000px|align-cap=center|[[ETO Park]] panoramaalign-cap=center|}}
The home of the club is the [[multi-purpose stadium]] [[ETO Park]]. It was opened in 2008 as a replacement for [[Stadion ETO]]. Its capacity is 16,000 but if necessary the stadium can be expanded in order to host 30,000 spectators. The stadium complex also includes three grass practice pitches and one synthetic practice pitch, as well as two indoor pitches. A hotel can be found next to the pitch. The stadium can also host cultural events such as concerts and exhibitions. The style of the stadium is 'a la English' that is open at the corners.

The stadium is also home to the national team of [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]]. On 3 March 2010 [[Hungary national football team|Hungary]] drew with [[Russia national football team|Russia]] in a friendly match. 16,000 spectators attended the match.

On 29 February 2012, Hungary hosted [[Bulgaria national football team|Bulgaria]] in a friendly match. Although [[Ádám Szalai|Szalai]] scored in the 42nd minute, a late equalizer was delivered by [[Valeri Bozhinov|Bozhinov]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/friendlies/season=2012/matches/round=2000293/match=2009350/index.html|title=Hungary 1-1 Bulgaria=UEFA.com|date=29 February 2012|accessdate=29 February 2012}}</ref>

On 7 June 2013, Csaba Tarsoly said that the [[ETO Park]] is unique in [[Hungary]] that it was built without any governmental support. The facility corresponds to the [[UEFA stadium categories|UEFA stadium criteria]], except for the fact that a [[UEFA Champions League]] or [[UEFA Europa League]] final cannot be played at the stadium since it is not able to host 30,000 spectators.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/nb-i-2014-ben-lesz-stadion-debrecenben-es-az-ulloi-uton-2251727|title=NB I: 2014-ben lesz stadion Debrecenben és az Üllői úton|date=7 June 2013|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref>

==Ownership==
On 14 June 2001, the Quaestor Financial Hrurira Kft bought the club and the stadium from the Rába Rt.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sportgeza.hu/hirek/48048/|title=Eladták a Rába ETO stadionját|date=14 June 2001|publisher=Sport Géza}}</ref>

On 12 May 2013, [[Csaba Tarsoly]], the president of the club, said that he had been waiting for this winning of the [[Hungarian League]] for 12  years.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/gyori-eto-a-klubelnok-regota-vart-mar-erre-a-sikerre-2244359|title=Győri ETO: a klubelnök régóta várt már erre a sikerre|date=12 May 2013|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref>

On 9 March 2015, the Quaestor Financial Hrurira [[Korlátolt felelősségű társaság|Kft.]] went bankrupt.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/uj-korszak-johet-gyorben-az-onkormanyzat-figyel-2400117|title=Új korszak jöhet Győrben&nbsp;– az önkormányzat figyel|date=11 March 2015|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref>

On 14 March 2015, the [[Audi]] finished supporting the club due to the unpredictability caused by the bankruptcy of the Quaestor Financial Hrurira [[Korlátolt felelősségű társaság|Kft.]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/gyori-eto-az-audi-kihatral-a-futballcsapat-mogul-2400635|title=Győri ETO: az Audi kihátrál a futballcsapat mögül|date=14 March 2015|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref>

On 22 April 2015, the mayor of [[Győr]], [[Zsolt Borkai]] said that the club will compete either in the [[Nemzeti Bajnokság III]] or in the county championship from the 2015–16 season due to the fact that the club will not be able to receive license from the [[Hungarian Football Federation]] since the club has amassed a 200 million HUF of debt.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/eto-kulfoldi-befekteto-a-lathataron-folynak-a-targyalasok-2408927|title=ETO: külföldi befektető a láthatáron, folynak a tárgyalások|date=22 April 2015|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://nol.hu/sport/beszanthatjak-az-eto-parkot-400-gyerek-kerulhet-az-utcara-1529403|title=Beszánthatják az ETO Parkot, 400 gyerek kerülhet az utcára|date=21 April 2015|publisher=[[Népszabadság]]}}</ref>

On 23 April 2015, it was revealed that an English investor is interested in the club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/eto-az-angol-befektetojelolt-majus-8-ig-kivar-2409263|title=ETO: az angol befektetőjelölt május 8-ig kivár|date=22 April 2015|publisher=[[Nemzeti Sport]]}}</ref>

==Supporters and rivalries==
[[File:Ultras Győr.jpg|thumb|right|250px|The ultras of Győr in Hungarian League match against Videoton (2010).]]
The supporters of the club are mainly from the city of [[Győr]] and the neighbouring area. However,the club have supporters from all over the country.
Győr have a strong local rivalry with neighbouring clubs such as [[Szombathelyi Haladás]] and [[Zalaegerszegi TE|Zalaegerszeg]]. However, the club's historic rivals come from [[Budapest]], due to the fierce competition between Győr, [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]] and [[Budapest Honvéd FC|Budapest Honvéd]] in the early 1980s. Currently the club are in rivalry with the most successful clubs of the country such as [[Debreceni VSC|Debrecen]] and [[Videoton FC|Videoton]].

==Other departments==
Győri ETO has several successful departments, e.g., the [[Team handball|handball]] teams won many national championships. The men's team also won the [[EHF Cup]] in 1986 as ''Rába Vasas ETO Győr''. Particularly well known these days are the handball ladies, playing as [[Győri Audi ETO KC|Győri ETO Kézilabda Club]] (en. ''handball club''), which made it in 2006 into the final of the [[Women's EHF Cup Winners' Cup|Cup Winners' Cup]] and in 2007 into the semifinals of the [[Women's EHF Champions League|Champions League]].
