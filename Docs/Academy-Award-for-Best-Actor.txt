The '''Academy Award for Best Actor''' is an award presented annually by the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS). It is given in honor of an actor who has delivered an outstanding performance in a [[Lead actor|leading]] role while working within the [[film industry]].

The [[1st Academy Awards]] ceremony was held in 1929 with [[Emil Jannings]] receiving the award for his roles in ''[[The Last Command (1928 film)|The Last Command]]'' and ''[[The Way of All Flesh (1927 film)|The Way of All Flesh]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf|page=1|format=PDF|title=Rule One: Award Definitions | accessdate=August 30, 2013|publisher=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS)}}</ref> Currently, nominees are determined by [[single transferable vote]] within the actors branch of AMPAS; winners are selected by a [[Plurality (voting)|plurality vote]] from the entire eligible voting members of the Academy.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf|page=8-7|format=PDF|title=Rule Six: Special Rules for the Acting Awards | accessdate=August 30, 2013|publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS)}}</ref>

In the first three years of the awards, actors were nominated as the best in their categories. At that time, all of their work during the qualifying period (as many as three films, in some cases) was listed after the award.<ref name="Oscar history">{{harvnb|Levy|2003|p=56}}</ref> However, during the [[3rd Academy Awards|3rd ceremony]] held in 1930, only one of those films was cited in each winner's final award, even though each of the acting winners had two films following their names on the ballots.<ref>{{cite news|last=Dirks|first=Tim|title=1929–38 Academy Awards Winners and History|url=http://www.filmsite.org/aa29.html|accessdate=August 30, 2013|work=[[Filmsite]]|publisher=[[Rainbow Media]]}}</ref> The following year, this unwieldy and confusing system was replaced by the current system in which an actor is nominated for a specific performance in a single film.<ref name="Oscar history" /> Starting with the [[9th Academy Awards|9th ceremony]] held in 1937, the category was officially limited to five nominations per year.<ref name="Oscar history" />

Since its inception, the award has been given to 79 actors. [[Daniel Day-Lewis]] has received the most awards in this category with three Oscars. [[Spencer Tracy]] and [[Laurence Olivier]] were nominated on nine occasions, more than any other actor. As of the [[88th Academy Awards|2016 ceremony]], [[Leonardo DiCaprio]] is the most recent winner in this category for his role as [[Hugh Glass]] in ''[[The Revenant (2015 film)|The Revenant]]''.

==Winners and nominees==
In the following table, the years are listed as per Academy convention, and generally correspond to the year of film release in [[Los Angeles County]]; the ceremonies are always held the following year.<ref>{{harvnb|Crouse|2005|p=257}}</ref> For the first five ceremonies, the eligibility period spanned twelve months from August 1 to July 31.<ref name="Oscar eligibility">{{harvnb|Levy|2003|p=52}}</ref> For the 6th ceremony held in 1934, the eligibility period lasted from August 1, 1932 to December 31, 1933.<ref name="Oscar eligibility" /> Since the 7th ceremony held in 1935, the period of eligibility became the full previous calendar year from January 1 to December 31.<ref name="Oscar eligibility" /> Rin Tin Tin received the most votes for Best Actor at the first Academy Award competition in 1929.<ref name=DogStar>{{cite news |last=Orlean |first=Susan |title=The Dog Star |url=http://www.newyorker.com/reporting/2011/08/29/110829fa_fact_orlean |accessdate=October 17, 2011 |newspaper=The New Yorker |date=August 29, 2011}}</ref>

==Notes==
{{refbegin}}
:'''A'''{{note|Jannings}}: According to longstanding Hollywood legend,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2011/08/29/the-dog-star |title=The Dog Star |first1=Susan|last1=Orlean |date=August 29, 2011 |work=[[The New Yorker]]|publisher=[[Condé Nast]] |accessdate=January 25, 2015}}</ref> reported by [[Susan Orlean]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/jan/04/award-oscar-dog-rin-tin-tin |title=Award posthumous Oscar to dog star Rin Tin Tin, says biographer |first1=Xan|last1=Brooks |date= |work=[[The Guardian]]|publisher=[[Guardian Media Group]] |accessdate=January 25, 2015}}</ref> [[Rin Tin Tin]] actually received the most Best Actor votes, but the Academy (not wishing to give the first award to a dog) refactored the votes to ensure that Jannings won.<ref>{{harvnb|Orlean|2011|pp=88–89}}</ref>
:'''B'''{{note label|Arliss||1}}{{note label|Arliss||2}}{{note label|Arliss||3}}: Rules at the time of the first three ceremonies allowed for a performer to receive a single nomination which could honor their work in more than one film. [[George Arliss]], [[Maurice Chevalier]], and [[Ronald Colman]] were all nominated for two different roles in the same category. Current Academy rules forbid this from happening. No official reason was ever given as to why Arliss won the award for only one of the two films he was listed for.<ref name="Variety Double Oscar Nominations">{{cite news|last=Rooney|first=David|title=On the double-bubble|url=http://variety.com/2003/film/news/on-the-double-bubble-1117896294/|work=[[Variety (magazine)|Variety]]|publisher=[[Penske Media Corporation]]|accessdate=August 30, 2013|date=November 25, 2003}}</ref>
:'''C'''{{note label|Tie||1}}{{note label|Tie||2}}: [[Fredric March]] received one more vote than [[Wallace Beery]]. Academy rules at that time considered such a close margin to be a tie, so both March and Beery received the award. Under current Academy rules, however, dual awards are given only for exact ties.<ref name="Oscar tie">{{harvnb|Levy|2003|p=116}}</ref>
:'''D'''{{note|Muni}}: [[Paul Muni]]'s performance in ''[[Black Fury (film)|Black Fury]]'' was not nominated for an Oscar. For two years only, the Academy allowed a write-in vote.<ref name="Write-in nomination3">{{harvnb|Wiley|Bona|1996|p=1002}}</ref><ref name="Write-in nomination4">{{harvnb|Wiley|Bona|1996|p=1045}}</ref> Technically, this meant that any performance was eligible for the award. The decision was made the previous year in response to the controversy surrounding [[Bette Davis]]'s failure to receive a nomination for ''[[Of Human Bondage]]''. Muni came 2nd in the vote, however, the Academy does not recognise Davis or Muni as nominees in these years.<ref name="Write-in nomination">{{harvnb|Levy|2003|p=51}}</ref><ref name="Write-in nomination2">{{harvnb|Wiley|Bona|1996|p=65}}</ref>
{{refend}}
