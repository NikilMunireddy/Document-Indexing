'''Cyclingnews.com''' is a website providing [[cycling]] news and race result owned by [[Immediate Media Company]].

==History==
In 1995 Australian [[Bill Mitchell (economist)|Bill Mitchell]], a keen cyclist and professor of economics at the [[University of Newcastle (Australia)|University of Newcastle]], created the website titled "Bill’s Cycling Racing Results and News" after finding there was a need for fast-breaking news and race results in English-speaking countries.<ref name="about">{{cite web|title=About Cyclingnews|url=http://ftp.cyclingnews.com/adpage/about.php|work=Cyclingnews.com|publisher=[[Future plc]]|location=Bath, UK|accessdate=4 April 2013}}</ref><ref name="cycling-tips">{{cite news|title=Cyclingnews {{!}} A Web 1.0 Success Story|url=http://www.cyclingtips.com.au/2011/08/cyclingnews-a-web-1-0-success-story/|work=Cycling Tips|accessdate=4 April 2013|date=8 August 2011}}</ref> In 1999 Sydney-based publishing company Knapp Communications purchased the website from Mitchell,<ref>{{cite news|last=Knapp|first=Gerard|title=It's been quite a climb, and now it's really time to ride|url=http://www.cyclingnews.com/editions/special-edition-cycling-news-july-4-2007|work=Cyclingnews.com|publisher=[[Future plc]]|location=Bath, UK|accessdate=4 April 2013|date=4 July 2007}}</ref> and in July 2007 they sold it to British publisher [[Future plc]] for £2.2m.<ref>{{cite web|title=Acquisition of www.cyclingnews.com|url=http://www.futureplc.com/2007/07/03/acquisition-of-www-cyclingnews-com/|work=[[Future plc]]|accessdate=5 April 2013|date=3 July 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Reid|first=Carlton|title=Future buys Cyclingnews.com for £2.2m|url=http://www.bikebiz.com/news/read/future-buys-cyclingnews-com-for-2-2m/0912|work=BikeBiz|publisher=Intent Media|accessdate=4 April 2013|location=Hertford, UK|date=3 July 2007}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Luft|first=Oliver|title=Future buys Cyclingnews.com for £2.2m|url=http://www.journalism.co.uk/news/future-buys-cyclingnews-com-for--2-2m/s2/a53389/|work=Journalism.co.uk|publisher=Mousetrap Media|location=Brighton, UK|accessdate=4 April 2013|date=3 July 2007}}</ref> In July 2014 it was bought by [[Immediate Media Company]]<ref>{{cite web|title=IMMEDIATE MEDIA CO COMPLETES ACQUISITION OF FUTURE CYCLING AND CRAFT
