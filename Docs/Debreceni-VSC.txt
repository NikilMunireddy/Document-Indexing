'''Debreceni Vasutas Sport Club''' is a [[Hungarian League]] professional [[association football|football]] club, based in [[Debrecen]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/teamsandplayers/teams/club=52958/domestic/|title=Debreceni VSC|date=15 July 2014|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> They are best known internationally for reaching the group stages of the [[UEFA Champions League 2009–10]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2010/clubs/index.html|title=UEFA Champions League 2009–10: Clubs|date=15 July 2014|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen have been the most successful club since 2000 by winning the Hungarian League seven times.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesh/hongchamp.html|title=Hungarian League winners|date=15 July 2014|publisher=The Rec Sport Soccer Statistics Foundation}}</ref>

==History==

===Early years (1902–1942)===
[[File:DVSC 1962-1963.jpeg|thumb|right|200px|Debrecen in 1962–63]]
The club was founded on 12 March 1902, initially being known as ''"Egyetértés Futball Club"'' but on becoming independent it was renamed ''"Debreceni Vasutas Sport Club" (Debrecen Railway Sports Club)''. In 1926 professionalism came to the Hungarian football scene, with the unfortunate result that [[Bocskai FC]] was formed using players from DVSC and city rivals DKASE and DTE, with the result that [[Bocskai FC]] ruled the football roost in Debrecen for 15 years while DVSC continued to struggle in the lower divisions. In 1940 the professional/amateur division of players was abolished and [[Bocskai FC]] suffered financial collapse, returning DVSC to top-dog position in the city. Debrecen reached the Hungarian First Division for the first time in 1942–43, but then suffered something of a [[yo-yo club|yo-yo]] existence for the next fifty years, suffering eight relegations in all, and even starting the 1967 season in the Third Division. During this period the club underwent a number of name changes. It became ''"Debreceni Vasutas Sport Egyesület"'' in 1948–49, ''"Debreceni Lokomotiv"'' (hence the nickname) in 1949, ''"Debreceni Törekvés"'' in the 1955–56 season, and it was known as ''"Debreceni Vasutas SC"'' from 1957 to 1979. In 1979 the two city teams, DVSC and DMTE were united into one team, called DMVSC, but after ten years the clubs split again and the team adopted its current name.

===Back to the First Division===
[[File:DVSC 1964.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Debrecen in 1964]]
[[File:DVSC 1964 (2).jpg|thumb|right|200px|Debrecen in 1964]]
Debrecen's most successful period has been since its most recent promotion to the First Division in 1993, since when the club has won its only national honours&nbsp;– the Hungarian Cup was won in 1999 and 2001, and the Hungarian League title for the first time in 2005. Although the team was relegated from top flight football in 2001 as a result of questionable refereeing on home matches (Ref. Béla Kiss was helped to escape from the stadium by officials in order to avoid the crowd's anger after the match against [[Budapest Honvéd FC]]), the then promoted [[BKV Előre SC]] could not afford to play in the first league in lack of the necessary financial conditions, thus DVSC remained in the top flight as the better relegated team according to the rules and regulations of the championship which led to some controversy whether it was just.<br>
The first international matches were played in 1998 in the [[Intertoto Cup]]. Debreceni VSC beat [[FC Dnepr Mogilev|Dnepr Mogilev]], [[FC Hradec Králové]], [[F.C. Hansa Rostock|Hansa Rostock]], and lost to [[Ruch Chorzów]] in the semi-finals.<br>
In 1999 Debrecen played in the first round of the [[UEFA Cup 1999-00]] and they were beaten by the German [[VfL Wolfsburg]] 3–2 on aggregate. Although Debrecen beat the German club 2–1 at home, they lost 2–0 in the away-game, and their first UEFA Cup season ended.

===2000s===
[[File:Bajnokavatas2007majus.jpg|thumb|300px|The third title of the [[Hungarian League]] in the [[Nemzeti Bajnokság I 2006-07|2006–07]] season]]
Debrecen won the [[2000–01 Magyar Kupa]]. Therefore in 2001 Debrecen they could enter the [[UEFA Cup 2001-02]] where they beat the Moldovan club [[FC Nistru Otaci|Nistru Otaci]] 3–1 on aggregate in the first round. In the second round of the UEFA Cup Debrecen played with the French club [[FC Girondins de Bordeaux|Bordeaux]]. They lost 5–1 away, but beat the French club 3–1 at home.

Debrecen finished third in the [[2002-03 Nemzeti Bajnokság I]] season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesh/hong03.html|title=Hungary 2002/03|date=1 June 2015|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation.com}}</ref> Therefore, they entered the [[UEFA Cup 2003-04]] season. Debrecen drew with the [[Lithuania]]n club [[FK Ekranas]] in the first leg of the qualifiers, while in the second leg the result was the same. After extra time Debrecen scored a goal and won 3–2 on aggregate. In the first round they had to face [[NK Varaždin|NK Varteks]]. Debrecen won both matches, 3–1 in the first leg and 3–2 in the second leg. The team won 6–3 on aggregate causing the resignation of the coach of the [[Croatia]]n club.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/news/newsid=111329.html|title=Bezek ousted at Varteks|date=26 September 2003|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the second round Debrecen faced with the [[Greeks|Greek]] [[PAOK FC|PAOK]]. In the first leg they drew 1–1 in the [[Toumba Stadium]] in [[Thessaloniki]], [[Greece]]. At home the result was a goalless draw, therefore Debrecen went through on away goals (1–1).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=124515.html|title=Szentes senses triumph|date=5 November 2003|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen's first successful UEFA Cup career was ended in the third round when they faced with the Belgian [[Club Brugge KV|Club Brugge]]. In the first leg Debrecen was beaten by 1–0 in [[Jan Breydel Stadium]], [[Brugge]], [[Belgium]]. The goalless second leg resulted the end of the march of the Hungarian club.

In the [[2003–04 Nemzeti Bajnokság I]] season Debrecen finished third, therefore they could play in the [[2004 UEFA Intertoto Cup|UEFA Intertoto Cup 2004–05]] season. In the first round Debrecen faced with the [[Slovaks|Slovak]] club [[FC Spartak Trnava|Spartak Trnava]]. In the first leg Debrecen lost 3–0 in [[Trnava]], [[Slovakia]], while at home they won 4–1. Debrecen were eliminated from the Intertoto Cup on away goals (4–4).

In 2005 Debrecen won their first [[Hungarian National Championship I 2004-05|Hungarian National Championship]] title preceding [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]] by six points.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/news/newsid=302946.html|title=First at last for Debrecen|date=20 May 2005|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> As a consequence the team could play in the [[UEFA Champions League]] for the first time. The first match in the [[UEFA Champions League 2005-06]] qualifiers was played in 2005 against the Croatian club, [[Hajduk Split]]. Debreceni VSC beat the Croatian rivals 8–0 on aggregate beating [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk Split]] 3–0 at home and 5–0 away. In the next round Debreceni VSC had to face one of the best teams in Europe, [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] and lost 0–6 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=324529.html|title=United seek summer solace|date=9 August 2005|publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=329610.html|title=United numbed by Neville loss|date=24 August 2005|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen could have consoled themselves in the [[2005–06 UEFA Cup]], but they had to face the Ukrainian club [[FC Shakhtar Donetsk|Shakhtar Donetsk]]. In the first leg (15 September 2005) the Ukrainian club beat Debrecen 4–1 in the [[RSC Olimpiyskiy]] stadium in [[Donetsk]], [[Ukraine]]. The only Hungarian goal was scored by [[Ibrahima Sidibe|Sidibe]]. In the second leg (29 September 2005) Debrecen lost 2–0 in the [[Stadion Oláh Gábor Út]]. Debrecen were eliminated 6–1 on aggregate.

Debrecen managed to duplicate the feat and win the [[Hungarian National Championship I 2005-06]], after in the last round on 3 June, they defeated [[Lombard-Pápa TFC|Pápa]] 4–1, and the then the leader of the league [[Újpest FC|Újpest]] (who had the same amount of points as Debrecen but had more away wins) lost to [[Videoton FC|Fehérvár]] in a climatic battle ending in 3–1, which also caused Fehérvár to place 3rd.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=427266.html|title=Debrecen clinch title at the death|date=3 June 2006|publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=430220.html|title=Debrecen did it again|date=19 June 2006|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

[[File:Debrecen-Rabotnicki.jpg|thumb|right|300px|[[UEFA Champions League 2006-07]] Qualifying match between Debrecen and [[Rabotnicki Skopje]]]]
In the qualifiers of the [[2006–07 UEFA Champions League qualifying rounds|2006–07 UEFA Champions League]] Debrecen faced with Macedonian champion [[FK Rabotnički|Rabotnički Skopje]]. In the first leg (26 July 2006) Debrecen drew at home in the [[Stadion Oláh Gábor Út]]. the Hungarian goal was scored by [[Róbert Zsolnai]]. In the second leg (2 August 2006) Debrecen took the lead by a goal by [[Ibrahim Sidibe|Sidibe]] in the 20th minute, but Rabotnicki scored four more goals. Debrecen were eliminated from the UEFA Champions League on 5–2 aggregate. After being eliminated from the Champions League [[Attila Supka|Supka]] resigned <ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=443560.html|title=Supka steps down at Debrecen|date=9 August 2006|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In the [[Hungarian National Championship I 2006-07]] season Debrecen became again champion on the 28th match-day beating [[Rákospalotai EAC]] on home turf. Debrecen became the first non-Budapest team winning the Hungarian title for the third time in a row.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=547106.html|title=Debrecen sign off in style|date=4 June 2007|publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=538733.html|title=Debrecen awaits victory parade|date=16 May 2007|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

[[András Herczeg]]'s first trophy was won against [[Budapest Honvéd FC|Budapest Honvéd]] by 4–1 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=561313.html|title=Debrecen claim third Super Cup|date=16 July 2006|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> The first leg finished 1–1 in the [[Bozsik Stadion]]. In the second leg [[Balázs Dzsudzsák|Dzsudzsák]] scored twice, while the third goals was scored by [[Zoltán Kiss (footballer born 1986)|Kiss]].

In the [[UEFA Champions League 2007-08]] season Debrecen entered in the second qualifying round. Debrecen faced with the [[Swedish people|Swedish]] champion [[IF Elfsborg|Elfsborg]]. The Swedish club won the first leg by 1–0, while a goalless draw in [[Sweden]] was enough for Elfsborg to go through the third qualifying round. Debreceni VSC were eliminated from the [[UEFA Champions League]] 1–0 on aggregate.

In the [[2007–08 Nemzeti Bajnokság I]] season Debrecen finished second while MTK became first.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=516350.html|title=Debrecen shining in Hungary|date=14 March 2007|publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=701466.html|title=MTK claim title after five-year wait|date=26 May 2008|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen won the [[2007–08 Magyar Kupa]] season by beating [[Budapest Honvéd FC|Budapest Honvéd]] in the final 9–1 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=705669.html|title=Debrecen enjoy easy cup win|date=4 June 2008|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Given the fact that Debrecen finished second in the Hungarian League and won the Hungarian Cup, the club qualified for the [[UEFA Cup 2008-09]] season. Debrecen had to enter the first qualifying round.

Since Debrecen could not win the Hungarian Championship, they had to play in the [[UEFA Cup 2008-09]]. In the first round of the Central-East region they drew in [[Kazakhstan]] against [[FC Shakhter Karagandy|Shakhter Karagandy]] 1–1, while in the second leg they beat the [[Kazakhs|Kazakh]] team by 1–0. Debrecen qualifyied for the second round by 2–1 on aggregate. In the second round Debrecen faced with the Swiss [[BSC Young Boys|Young Boys]]. In the first leg they lost 4–1 in [[Bern]]. In the second leg they lost 3–2 at home. Debrecen were eliminated from the [[UEFA Cup]] by 3–7 on aggregate.

Debrecen won the [[Hungarian National Championship I 2008–09]] after beating their provincial rivals [[Diósgyőri VTK|Diósgyőr]] 3–2 away. Debrecen became the most successful non-Budapest team in Hungary with four Hungarian Championship titles.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=832568.html|title=Debrecen wrap up Hungarian honours|date=23 May 2009|publisher=UEFA}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=837287.html|title=Season review: Hungary|date=10 June 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

On 15 July 2009 Debreceni VSC beat [[Kalmar FF]] 2–0 at home by the goals of [[Zoltán Kiss (footballer born 1986)|Kiss]] and [[József Varga (footballer born 1988)|Varga]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/news/newsid=848926.html|title=Debrecen wary of Kalmar comeback|date=22 July 2009|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the away match Debrecen lost to the Swedish champion by 3–1, but they qualified for the next round because they won on away goals. In the third round of the [[UEFA Champions League|Champions League]] Debrecen played their first match in [[Estonia]] against [[FC Levadia Tallinn|Levadia Tallinn]] on 29 July 2009. Debrecen won ths match thanks to the 70th-minute goal of [[Leandro Marcolini Pedroso de Almeida|Leandro]]. At home in [[Debrecen]] on 5 August 2009, the team beat the [[Estonians|Estonian]] champion by 1–0. In the play-offs of the [[2009–10 UEFA Champions League|Champions League 2009–10]] Debrecen faced with the Bulgarian champion [[PFC Levski Sofia|Levski Sofia]]. The first match was played in [[Sofia]] on 19 August 2009, where Debrecen beat the Bulgarian club by 2–1. Although [[PFC Levski Sofia|Levski Sofia]] scored an equalizer, Debrecen were able to respond with a goal which came in the 76th minute by [[Péter Czvitkovics|Czvitkovics]]. Debrecen won the first leg of the play-off in [[Bulgaria]]. Debrecen beat [[PFC Levski Sofia|Levski Sofia]] by 2–0 in front of 32,000 spectators and won 4–1 on aggregate. They were drawn in group E of the competition alongside [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]], [[Olympique Lyonnais|Lyon]] and [[ACF Fiorentina|Fiorentina]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/competitions/ucl/news/kind=1/newsid=878128.html|title=Inter lie in wait for holders Barça|date=27 August 2009|work=UEFA.com|accessdate=2009-08-27}}</ref> Debrecen played their first ever Champions League group match against Liverpool at [[Anfield]]. The only goal of the match was scored by [[Dirk Kuyt|Kuyt]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/europe/8254451.stm|title=Liverpool 1–0 Debrecen|date=16 September 2009|work=UEFA.com|accessdate=2009-09-16}}</ref> Although Debrecen were unable to get points from the group matches, their supporters could experience the atmosphere of the Champions League.

===2010s===
In 2010 Debrecen was competing for the title with [[Videoton FC|Videoton]] In the last round the difference between the two teams were only one point. Videoton had to beat [[Győri ETO FC|Győri ETO]] away, while Debreceni VSC had to beat [[Kecskeméti TE]] away. Neither Videoton nor Debrecen were able to win in the last round, therefore the difference remained one point. As a consequence, Debreceni VSC won the [[Hungarian National Championship I 2009–10]] despite losing to Kecskeméti TE in the last round.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1503384.html|title=Season review: Hungary|date=9 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In 2010 Debrecen beat [[Zalaegerszegi TE]] in the [[Magyar Kupa|Hungarian Cup]] final in the [[Puskás Ferenc Stadium]] by 3–2.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1492865.html|title=Debrecen complete double with Hungarian Cup|date=26 May 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

In 2010 Debrecen beat [[FC Levadia Tallinn|Levadia Tallinn]] 4–3 on aggregate in the second round of the [[UEFA Champions League 2010–11]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/matches/season=2011/round=2000115/match=2002435/index.html|title=Levadia push Debrecen all the way|date=21 July 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> In the third round they lost to [[FC Basel]] 5–1 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/matches/season=2011/round=2000116/match=2002735/postmatch/report/|title=Debrecen well beaten by Basel|date=4 August 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen were eliminated yet again. Debrecen consoled themselves in the [[UEFA Europa League 2010–11]]. Debrecen beat [[PFC Litex Lovech|Litex Lovech]] 4–1 on aggregate reaching the group stages of the Europa League.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/matches/season=2011/round=2000127/match=2003102/index.html|title=Litex 1–2 Debrecen|date=26 August 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Debrecen were drawn in group I of the competition alongside [[PSV Eindhoven]], [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]], and [[FC Metalist Kharkiv|Metalist Kharkiv]]. Debrecen finally obtained their first points in the [[Europa League]]. [[Péter Kabát]]'s two goals were enough to beat [[U.C. Sampdoria|Sampdoria]] by 2–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/matches/season=2011/round=2000128/match=2003037/prematch/background/|title=Debrecen eager to avoid null points|date=4 December 2010|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> However, these three point were not enough to qualify for the knockout phase of the [[Europa League 2010-11]].

[[Elemér Kondás]] was appointed as the new manager of the club on 20 April 2011. In the [[2011–12 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2011–12]] the team started with a seven consecutive wins. The record run was stopped by a draw against [[Siófok KC|Siófok]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/leaguematchcentre/index.html#date=2012/05/12league=1063match=|title=Siófok 0–0 Debrecen|publisher=UEFA.com|date=9 September 2011|accessdate=10 September 2011}}</ref> On 1 May 2012 Debrecen beat MTK Budapest on [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]] in the [[2011–12 Magyar Kupa|2011–12]] season of the [[Hungarian Cup]] final winning the trophy for the fifth time.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1792219.html|title=Debrecen lift cup after shoot-out success|publisher=UEFA.com|date=2 May 2012|accessdate=2 May 2012}}</ref> On 12 May 2012 Debrecen beat [[Pécsi MFC|Pécs]] in the 28th round of the Hungarian League by 4–0 at the Oláh Gábor út Stadium which resulted the sixth Hungarian League title for the [[Hajdú-Bihar County|Hajdús]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/leaguematchcentre/index.html#date=2012/05/12league=1063match=|title=Debrecen 4–0 Pécs|publisher=UEFA.com|date=12 May 2012|accessdate=13 May 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1797002.html|title=Debrecen crowned champions of Hungary|publisher=UEFA.com|date=12 May 2012|accessdate=13 May 2012}}</ref> Debrecen finished the 2011–12 season of the Hungarian League unbeaten with 22 victories and only 8 draws.

In the [[UEFA Champions League 2012–13]] season Debrecen faced the Albanian champions [[KF Skënderbeu Korçë|Skënderbeu Korçë]] in the second round of the qualifiers. The first leg was played in [[Korçë]] and won by the Albanian champions 1–0,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000344/match=2009404/index.html|title=Skënderbeu eke out Debrecen victory|publisher=UEFA.com|date=17 July 2012|accessdate=17 July 2012}}</ref> while the second leg was played in [[Nyíregyháza]] (the home of [[Nyíregyháza Spartacus FC|Nyíregyháza Spartacus]]) since [[Stadion Oláh Gábor Út]] did not meet UEFA requirements. In the second leg [[Adamo Coulibaly|Coulibaly]] scored twice, while [[József Varga (footballer born 1988)|Varga]] resulting the 3–1 victory on aggregate over Skënderbeu.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000344/match=2009421/index.html|title=Debrecen come from behind to see off Skënderbeu|publisher=UEFA.com|date=24 July 2012|accessdate=24 July 2012}}</ref> In the third round Debrecen faced the [[Belarusian Premier League|Belarus]] champions [[FC BATE Borisov|BATE Borisov]]. The first leg was played in [[Borisov (Belarus)|Borisov]] and ended with [[Ibrahima Sidibe|Sidibe]]'s two goals, one for Debrecen and a late own goal in the 93rd minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000345/match=2009439/postmatch/report/index.html|title=BATE earn late draw against Debrecen|publisher=UEFA.com|date=1 August 2012|accessdate=1 August 2012}}</ref> The second leg was won by the Belarus club 2–0 in Nyíregyháza which resulted the farewell of the club from the UEFA Champions League 2012–13 season.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000345/match=2009454/index.html|title=BATE come good in Debrecen second leg|publisher=UEFA.com|date=7 August 2012|accessdate=7 August 2012}}</ref> Hungarian defender [[Balázs Nikolov|Nikolov]] was sent off in the 57th minute which resulted the breakdown of Debrecen. The adieu from the Champions League meant an entry to the play-offs of the [[UEFA Europa League 2012–13]] season. Debrecen faced with the Belgian club [[Club Brugge K.V.|Club Brugge]] who they met in the early 2000s. The first leg was played in Nyíregyháza and the match was won by Brugge 3–0.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000355/match=2009838/index.html|title=Debrecen 0–3 Club Brugge|publisher=UEFA.com|date=23 August 2012|accessdate=23 August 2012}}</ref> Debrecen could withstand the attacks of the Belgian club until the expulsion of [[Luis Ramos (Honduran footballer)|Luis Ramos]] in the 45th minute. In the second leg Debrecen lost 4–1<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2013/matches/round=2000355/match=2009869/index.html|title=Club Brugge 4–1 Club Brugge|publisher=UEFA.com|date=30 August 2012|accessdate=30 August 2012}}</ref> in the [[Jan Breydel Stadium]], [[Brugge]]. [[László Rezes|Rezes]]'s expulsion in the 40th minute resulted a hopeless second half for the club. Debrecen were eliminated 7–1 on aggregate.

On 22 May 2013, Győr could have double their success by winning the [[2012–13 Magyar Kupa]] against Debrecen at the [[Bozsik Stadion]], [[Budapest]]. Győr was winning by 1–0 thanks to the goal by [[Nemanja Andrić]] in the 19th minute but in the second half Debrecen's [[Adamo Coulibaly]] scored two goals which resulted Debrecen's victory over Győr in the Hungarian Cup final.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/association=hun/news/newsid=1955271.html|title=Debreceni deny Győr in Hungarian Cup final|date=22 May 2013|publisher=UEFA}}</ref>

On 18 July Debrecen played against [[Strømsgodset IF|Strømsgodset]] in the second qualifying round of the [[UEFA Europa League 2013–14]] season. The first leg ended in a 2–2 tie in [[Drammen]], [[Norway]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2014/matches/round=2000466/match=2012291/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League 2013–14: Stromsgodset 2–2 Debrecen|date=18 July 2013|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> The second leg was played on 25 July 2013 at the [[Városi Stadion (Nyíregyháza)|Városi Stadion]] in [[Nyíregyháza]] since Debrecen's stadium was under construction. The match ended in a 3–0 loss for the local team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefaeuropaleague/season=2014/matches/round=2000466/match=2012331/index.html|title=UEFA Europa League 2013–14: Debrecen 0–3 Stromsgodset|date=25 July 2013|publisher=UEFA}}</ref> Interestingly enough, the Hungarian [[Péter Kovács (footballer)|Péter Kovács]] of Stromsgodset scored twice in the two-legged encounter.

Debrecen won the [[2013–14 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2013–14]] season of the [[Hungarian League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesh/hong2014.html|title=Hungarian League 2013–14|date=25 May 2014|publisher=The Rec Sport Soccer Statistics Foundation}}</ref> As a consequence, Debrecen were eligible for entering the second round of the [[UEFA Champions League 2014–15]] season. On 15 July 2014, the first leg ended with a goalless drew against [[Cliftonville F.C.|Cliftonville]] at [[Solitude (football ground)|Solitude in]] [[Belfast]], [[Northern Ireland]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/28278524|title=Cliftonville 0–0 Debrecen|date=15 July 2014|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]}}</ref>

In the 2014–15 Nemzeti Bajnokság I season, Debrecen finished fourth.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesh/hong2015.html#nb1|title=Hungary 2014/15|date=5 July 2015|publisher=The Rec Sport Soccer Statistics Foundation}}</ref> Debrecen were eligible for entering the [[2015–16 UEFA Europa League]] season. In the first round, Debrecen beat 3–0 the [[Montenegrins|Montenegrin]] [[FK Sutjeska Nikšić]] at the [[Nagyerdei Stadion]] on 2 July 2015.

==Stadiums and Facilities==
{{Main|Stadion Oláh Gábor Út}}
[[File:Debrecen-Rabotnicki.jpg|thumb|right|250px|Debrecen played their home matches at [[Stadion Oláh Gábor Út]] between 1989 and 2014]]
Debrecen played their home matches between 1989 and 2014 at the [[Stadion Oláh Gábor Út|Stadium Oláh Gábor út]], which has a capacity of 12,500. After winning the first [[Nemzeti Bajnokság I]] trophy, it was found that the stadium did not meet [[UEFA stadium categories|UEFA stadium criteria]]. Therefore, the most important [[UEFA Champions League]] and [[UEFA Europa League]] matches had to be played either at the [[Ferenc Puskás Stadium]] as in [[2009-10 UEFA Champions League group stage]] and the [[2010-11 UEFA Europa League group stage]].

{{Main|Nagyerdei Stadion}}
[[File:Nagyerdei stadium.jpg|thumb|right|250px|[[Nagyerdei Stadion]] was opened in 2014]]
On 1 May 2014, the Nagyerdei stadium was opened with an inauguration ceremony. [[Viktor Orbán]], [[Hungarian prime minister]], said that "the Hungarian spirit, the architects, the engineers, the workers make Hungary a big country again with this constructions like the Nagyerdei stadium" (in Hungarian: "A magyar szellem, a tervezők, a mérnökök, a munkások ilyen alkotásokkal teszik ismét naggyá Magyarországot"). The stadium was also santificied by Nándor Bosák, bishop of the [[Roman Catholic Church]], Gusztáv Bölcskei, [[Minister (Christianity)|minister]], and Fülöp Kocsis, bishops of the [[Greek Catholic Church]]. After the [[sanctification]], singers such as [[Lou Bega]], [[Viktor Király]], Ildikó Kersztes, Gigi Radić, and [[Erika Miklósa]] entertained the audience. The first match was played between the Debrecen All Stars and [[Hungary national football team|Hungary All Stars]], including players such as [[Attila Pintér (footballer born 1966)|Attila Pintér]], current [[Hungary national football team]] coach,  [[Kálmán Kovács]], former [[Budapest Honvéd FC|Budapest Honvéd]] legend, [[Péter Lipcsei]], former [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]] icon, [[György Véber]], former [[Újpest FC|Újpest]] icon and current [[Mezőkövesd-Zsóry SE|Mezőkövesd]] manager, [[Imre Garaba]], former Hungary international, [[Flórián Urbán]], former Újpest legend, and [[Lajos Détári]], former Hungary national team legend.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/dvsc-unnep-debrecenben-atadtak-a-nagyerdei-stadiont-2332803|title=DVSC: átadták a vadonatúj Nagyerdei Stadiont&nbsp;– fotók|date=1 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref>

On 10 May 2014, the first official match was played at the new stadium between Debrecen and [[Újpest FC|Újpest]] in the [[2013–14 Nemzeti Bajnokság I|2013–14 Hungarian League]] season. The match ended with a 3–1 victory over the [[Budapest]]-based rival, Újpest. The first goal of the match was scored by [[Tamás Kulcsár|Kulcsár]] in the 27th minute. Although [[Dušan Vasiljević|Vasiljević]] equalised the score in the 37th minute, Debrecen were able to clinch the victory in the second half due to an own goal by [[Chema Antón|Antón]] in the 46 minute and a goal by [[Dániel Vadnai|Vadnai]] in the 85th minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/dvsc-unnep-debrecenben-atadtak-a-nagyerdei-stadiont-2332803|title=Matematikailag nem, gyakorlatilag viszont már bajnok lehet a Loki|date=9 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/labdarugo_nb_i/dvsc-loki-fieszta-az-uj-stadion-nyitanyan-kepek-2334955|title=DVSC: Loki-fieszta az új stadion nyitányán&nbsp;– képek|date=10 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref>

On 22 May 2014, the first match of the [[Hungary national football team]] was played at the stadium in front of 20,000 spectators, which ended with a 2–2 draw against [[Denmark national football team]]. The first goal was scored by the former Debrecen player [[Balázs Dzsudzsák|Dzsudzsák]]. [[Christian Eriksen|Eriksen]] equalised the score in the 56th minute. The debutant [[Roland Varga (footballer)|Varga]] took the lead in the 69th minute again, but the score was equalised by [[Lasse Schöne|Schöne]] in the 72nd minute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/magyar_valogatott/magyarorszag-dania-valogatott-merkozes-2337529|title=Kétszer is vezettünk a dánok ellen, szép gólok után a vége iksz|date=22 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/magyar_valogatott/valogatott-gyengek-voltunk-az-eb-re-keszulo-danoknak-lapszemle-2337687|title=Válogatott: a dánok szerint gyenge csapattal ikszeltek&nbsp;– lapszemle|date=22 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nemzetisport.hu/magyar_valogatott/a-mosdoban-volt-melegviz-es-egy-orat-meg-futballoztunk-is-2337651|title=A mosdóban volt meleg víz, és egy órát még futballoztunk is!|date=22 May 2014|publisher=www.nso.hu}}</ref>

==Supporters==
Supporters of DVSC are mainly based in [[Debrecen]], Hungary. However, the club is popular in all over Hungary. Club's main [[ultras]] group is ''Szívtiprók Ultras Debrecen'' (SZ.U.D.), which is the largest and oldest faction, founded in 1994.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://szud94.hu/|title=Szívtiprók Ultras Debrecen|date=3 August 2015|publisher=szud94.hu}}</ref> As of 2000, SZ.U.D started to divide itself, in factions of ultras outside Debrecen, such as ''SZ.U.D. Budapest'', ''SZ.U.D. Nagyvárad'' and ''SZ.U.D. Hódmezővásárhely''. Among smaller groups are considered ''Force Field'', ''Red Territory'' and ''Vadmacskák'', which are the oldest. However, since 2009, new ultras factions, such as ''Sziporkák'', ''Debrecen Hooligans'', ''Barrabrava Debrecen'' and others appeared. This while several, like ''North Side Hooligans'' were dissolved in early 2000s. Debrecen has fierce rivalries with [[Nyíregyháza Spartacus FC|Nyíregyháza]], [[Békéscsaba 1912 Előre SE|Békéscsaba]] and competitive rivalries with [[Ferencvárosi TC|Ferencváros]], [[Újpest FC|Újpest]] and [[Videoton FC|Videoton]].

On 5 August 2014, UEFA issued sanctions against Debrecen, [[Romania]]'s [[FC Steaua București|Steaua București]] and [[Slovenia]]'s [[NK Maribor|Maribor]] following racist behaviour by their fans during [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League qualifying phase and play-off round|2014–15 UEFA Champions League qualifying]] against [[Cliftonville FC|Cliftonville]], [[Strømsgodset IF|Strømsgodset]] and [[Zrinjski Mostar]] respectively. Debrecen shut sector B of their ground after fans showed an "illicit banner" during the match against [[Northern Ireland]]’s Cliftonville.<ref>[http://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/aug/05/uefa-partial-stadium-bans-racist-behaviour UEFA closes stands at Steaua, Debrecen and Maribor after racist incidents], The Guardian, 5 August 2015</ref>
