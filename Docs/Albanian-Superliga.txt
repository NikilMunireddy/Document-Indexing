The '''Albanian Superliga''' ({{lang-sq|Kategoria Superiore}}), is a professional league for men's [[association football]] clubs. At the top of the Albanian football league system, it is the country's primary football competition. Currently contested by 10 clubs, it operates on a system of promotion and relegation with the [[Albanian First Division]]. Seasons currently run from August to May, with teams playing 36 matches each (playing each team in the league four times, twice at home and twice away).

The competition was originally founded in 1930 as the '''Albanian National Championship''' during the reign of [[King Zog]], shortly after the creation of the [[Albanian Football Association]]. Since 1930, 43 clubs have competed in officially recognized competitions, while only 8 clubs have won the title: [[KF Tirana]] (24), [[FK Dinamo Tirana|Dinamo Tirana]] (18), [[FK Partizani Tirana|Partizani Tirana]] (15), [[KF Vllaznia Shkodër|Vllaznia Shkodër]] (9), [[KF Skënderbeu Korçë|Skënderbeu Korçë]] (7), [[KF Elbasani]] (1), [[Flamurtari Vlorë]] (1) and [[KF Teuta Durrës|Teuta Durrës]] (1). The current champions are [[KF Skënderbeu Korçë|Skënderbeu Korçë]], who won their 6th consecutive title in 2015–16.

== History ==

=== Early history ===
Football was first introduced to Albania by an English-Maltese priest named Gut Ruter, who visited the Saverian college in [[Shkodër]] in 1908. Arguably, the first football club in Albania was Indipendenca, and it was founded in [[Shkodër]] in 1912 by Palokë Nika.<ref name="Shkodra Sport">{{cite web|url=http://www.shkodrasport.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=3848%3A-paloke-nika-personazhi-historik-i-sportit-shqiptar&catid=45%3Apersonazhe&Itemid=108&lang|title=Palokë Nika&nbsp;– personazhi historik i sportit Shqiptar|date=|accessdate=2014-02-05|publisher=Shkodra Sport|author=Gjergj Kola|language=Albanian}}</ref> The first 90-minute game to be played with two 45 minute halves took place in October 1913 Indipendenca Shkodër and the occupying [[Austro-Hungarian Navy|Austro-Hungarian Imperial Navy]]. The game is considered to be the first international game to be played in Albania, and it ended in a 2–1 loss for Indipendenca, with the captain and founder of the club Palokë Nika scoring the only goal for the Albanians.<ref name="Shkodra Sport"/><ref name="Vllaznia">{{cite web|url=http://www.vllaznia.al/index.php/historia-e-vllaznise-shkoder/83-2013-09-13-19-54-09|title=Një historik i shkurtër i futbollit në Shkodër|date=|accessdate=2014-02-05|publisher=Vllaznia.al|author=|language=Albanian}}</ref>

The First Division in Albanian football was founded in 1930. The league has been dominated by clubs from [[Albania]]'s capital city, [[Tirana]], with only nineteen titles going elsewhere in the country. Nine of those nineteen titles have gone to [[KF Vllaznia Shkodër|Vllaznia Shkodër]]. {{citation needed|date=December 2015}}

=== Championships of World War II ===
{{main|1939 Albanian Superliga|1940 Albanian Superliga|1942 Albanian Superliga}}

[[Albania]] was among countries that played football during that period, even though in a limited way. Along this period, [[Albanian Football Association|AFA]] organized three championships, [[1939 Albanian Superliga|1939]], [[1940 Albanian Superliga|1940]] and [[1942 Albanian Superliga|1942]].<ref>[http://www.giovanniarmillotta.it/albania/calcio/championship.html RSSSF]</ref><ref>http://www.giovanniarmillotta.it/albania/calcio/trekampionate.html</ref>

The [[Albanian Football Association|AFA]] made a decision in February 2013 that the wartime championships are not legitimate and won't be recognized since they weren't organized by the [[Albanian Football Association|AFA]].<ref>[http://www.partizani.net/files/news.php?readmore=8820 AFA's General Assembly: War championships are not legitimate]  {{wayback|url=http://www.partizani.net/files/news.php?readmore=8820 |date=20140220023736 |df=y }}</ref>

== Current competition format ==
There are 10 clubs in the Superior League. During the course of the season (from August to May) each club plays the others 4 times, twice at their home stadium and twice at that of their opponents, for a total of 36 games. Teams receive three points for a win and one point for a draw. No points are awarded for a loss. Teams are ranked by total points, then head-to-head record, then goal difference, and then goals scored.  At the end of each season, the club with the most points are crowned champions. If points are equal, the head-to head record and then goal difference determine the winner. The two lowest placed teams are relegated to the [[Albanian First Division]] and the top two teams from there are promoted in their place. {{citation needed|date=May 2015}}

=== Qualification for European competitions ===
As of the start of the [[2013–14 Albanian Superliga]] season, qualification for European competitions is as follows: champions qualify for the 2nd qualifying round of the [[2014–15 UEFA Champions League|Champions League]], second and third place qualifies for the 1st qualifying round of the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. The [[Albanian Cup]] winners qualify for the 1st qualifying round of the [[2013–14 UEFA Europa League|Europa League]]. {{citation needed|date=May 2015}}
