==Events==

===January===
* [[January 1]]
** [[Bulgaria]] and [[Romania]] [[2007 enlargement of the European Union|join]] the [[European Union]]. Bulgarian, Romanian, and Irish become official languages of the European Union, joining 20 other official languages. [[Slovenia]] joins [[Eurozone]].
** South Korea's [[Ban Ki-moon]] becomes the new [[United Nations Secretary-General]], replacing [[Kofi Annan]].
** A [[Boeing 737|Boeing 737-4Q8]], operated by [[Adam Air]], disappeared from Jakarta's radar. A week later it was founded that the aircraft has [[Adam Air Flight 574|crashed]] onto the [[Makassar Strait]], killing all 102 people on board.
* [[January 8]] &ndash; Russian oil supplies to Poland, Germany, and [[Ukraine]] are cut as the [[2007 Russia–Belarus energy dispute|Russia–Belarus energy dispute]] escalates; they are restored 3 days later.
* [[January 9]] &ndash; [[Apple Inc.]]'s CEO and founder, [[Steve Jobs]], announces the [[iPhone (1st generation)|first generation iPhone]] (it goes on sale in the United States on [[June 29]]).
* [[January 12]] &ndash; Comet [[C/2006 P1]] (McNaught), the brightest comet in more than 40 years, makes [[perihelion]].
* [[January 13]] &ndash; The Greek ship ''Server'' breaks in half off the Norwegian coast, releasing over 200 tons of crude [[oil]].
* [[January 14]] &ndash; The [[International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement]] adopts the [[Emblems of the International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement|Red Crystal]] as a non-religious emblem for use in its overseas operations.
* [[January 17]] &ndash; Protests occur in India and the United Kingdom against the British series of [[Celebrity Big Brother 5|''Celebrity Big Brother'']], after [[Jade Goody]], [[Danielle Lloyd]] and [[Jo O'Meara]] were allegedly racially abusive towards [[Bollywood]] star [[Shilpa Shetty]].
* [[January 19]] &ndash; The [[State of Israel]] releases $100 million in frozen assets to President [[Mahmoud Abbas]] of the [[Palestinian National Authority]], in order to bolster the president's position.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://transcripts.cnn.com/TRANSCRIPTS/0612/23/tww.01.html |title=CNN.com &ndash; Transcripts |publisher=Transcripts.cnn.com |date= |accessdate=2008-10-06}}</ref>

===February===
* [[February 2]]
** [[Hu Jintao]], [[General Secretary of the Communist Party of China]] signs a series of economic deals with [[Sudan]].
** [[Martti Ahtisaari]] unveils a [[United Nations]] plan for the final status of [[Kosovo]]; [[Serbia]]n leaders denounce the proposal.
** The [[Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change|IPCC]] publishes its [[IPCC Fourth Assessment Report|fourth assessment report]], having concluded that global [[climate change]] is "very likely" to have a predominantly human cause.
* [[February 13]] &ndash; [[North Korea]] agrees to shut down its nuclear facilities in Yongbyon by April 14 as a first step towards complete denuclearization, receiving in return energy aid equivalent to 50,000 tons of heavy fuel oil.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://english.kbs.co.kr/news/newsview_sub.php?menu=1&key=2007021324 |title=KBS Global |publisher=English.kbs.co.kr |date= |accessdate=2011-05-21}}</ref>
* [[February 26]] &ndash; The [[International Court of Justice]] finds [[Serbia]] guilty of failing to prevent [[genocide]] in the [[Srebrenica massacre]], but clears it of direct responsibility and complicity in [[Bosnian Genocide case|the case]].
* [[February 27]] &ndash; [[The Chinese Correction]]: World [[stock market]]s plummet after China and Europe release less-than-expected growth reports.
* [[February 28]] &ndash; The [[New Horizons]] [[space probe]] makes a [[gravitational slingshot]] against [[Jupiter]], which changes its trajectory towards [[Pluto]].

===March===
* [[March 1]] &ndash; The [[International Polar Year]], a $1.73 billion research program to study both the [[North Pole]] and [[South Pole]], is launched in Paris.
* [[March 8]] &ndash; [[Israel]]i Prime Minister [[Ehud Olmert]] admits that Israel had planned an attack on [[Lebanon]] in the event of kidnapped soldiers on the border, months before [[Hezbollah]] carried out its kidnapping.
* [[March 23]] &ndash; Naval forces of [[Islamic Revolutionary Guards Corps|Iran's Revolutionary Guard]] [[2007 Iranian seizure of Royal Navy personnel|seize Royal Navy personnel]] in disputed Iran-Iraq waters.
* [[March 27]] &ndash; [[Prime Minister of Latvia]] [[Aigars Kalvītis]] and [[Prime Minister of Russia]] [[Mikhail Fradkov]] finally sign a border treaty between [[Latvia]] and Russia.

===April===
* [[April 3]] 
**French high speed passenger train, the TGV, reaches a top speed of 574.8&nbsp;km/h (357.2&nbsp;mph), breaking the record for the world's fastest conventional train.
**''Second [[Orange Revolution]]'': The [[President of Ukraine]], [[Viktor Yushchenko]], dissolves the [[Parliament of Ukraine|Ukrainian Parliament]], following defections that increased the majority of his opponents.
* [[April 4]] &ndash; ''[[2007 Iranian seizure of Royal Navy personnel]]'': The government of the [[Islamic Republic of Iran]] announces that they will release the group of imprisoned British sailors and Marines that were captured by them on [[March 23]].
* [[April 16]] &ndash; ''[[Virginia Tech shooting]]'': [[Seung-Hui Cho]], a South Korean expatriate student, shoots and kills 32 people at the [[Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University]], before committing suicide, resulting in the deadliest shooting incident by a single gunman in United States history.
* [[April 24]] &ndash; [[Gliese 581 c]], a potentially Earth-like extrasolar planet habitable for life, is discovered in the [[constellation]] [[Libra (constellation)|Libra]].
* [[April 26]]-[[April 27|27]] &ndash; ''[[Bronze Night]]'': [[Russians in Estonia|Ethnic Russians]] riot in [[Tallinn]] and other towns in [[Estonia]], about moving the [[Bronze Soldier of Tallinn|Bronze Soldier]], a Soviet World War II memorial. One person is killed after two of the worst nights of rioting in [[Estonia]]n history.

===May===
* [[May 3]] &ndash; British child [[Madeleine McCann]] disappears from an apartment in [[Praia da Luz]], Portugal.
* [[May 4]] &ndash; A deadly [[Enhanced Fujita scale|EF5]] [[May 2007 tornado outbreak|tornado]] destroys much of [[Greensburg, Kansas]].
* [[May 16]] &ndash; The [[United Nations]] [[United Nations General Assembly|General Assembly]], recognizing that genuine [[multilingualism]] promotes unity in diversity and international understanding, proclaims [[2008]] the [[International Year of Languages]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.un.org/News/Press/docs/2007/ga10592.doc.htm |title=GENERAL ASSEMBLY PROCLAIMS 2008 INTERNATIONAL YEAR OF LANGUAGES, IN EFFORT TO PROMOTE UNITY IN DIVERSITY, GLOBAL UNDERSTANDING |publisher=Un.org |date= |accessdate=2011-05-21| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20110513095941/http://www.un.org/News/Press/docs/2007/ga10592.doc.htm| archivedate= 13 May 2011 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref>
* [[May 17]] &ndash; The [[Russian Orthodox Church Abroad]] and the [[Moscow Patriarchate]] re-unite after 80 years of schism.
* [[May 20]] &ndash; Sheikh [[Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum]] of [[Dubai]] makes the largest single charitable donation in modern history, committing €7.41 billion to [[Mohammed bin Rashid Al Maktoum Foundation|an educational foundation]] in the Middle East.

===June===
* [[June 1]] &ndash; A 2,100-year-old melon is discovered by archaeologists in western Japan.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20070601/ap_on_fe_st/japan_oldest_melon;_ylt=ApRgC.q7xY7jDAOUFbldew0PLBIF |title=Researchers Find 2,100 Year Old Melon |publisher=Yahoo News |accessdate=2007-06-01 |deadurl=yes |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/20081218151104/http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20070601/ap_on_fe_st/japan_oldest_melon;_ylt=ApRgC.q7xY7jDAOUFbldew0PLBIF |archivedate=December 18, 2008 }}</ref>
* [[June 5]] &ndash; [[NASA]]'s [[MESSENGER]] spacecraft makes its second fly-by of [[Venus]] en route to [[Mercury (planet)|Mercury]].
* [[June 28]] &ndash; [[2007 European heat wave]]: in the aftermath of Greece's worst [[heat wave]] in a century, at least 11 people are reported dead from [[heatstroke]], approximately 200 [[2007 Greek forest fires|wildfires break out nationwide]], and the country's electricity grid nearly collapses due to record breaking demand.

===July===
* [[July 2]] &ndash; [[Venus]] and [[Saturn]] are in [[conjunction (astronomy)|conjunction]], separation 46 arcsecs.
* [[July 7]] &ndash; [[Live Earth]] Concerts are held throughout 9 major cities around the [[world]].
* [[July 17]] &ndash; [[TAM Airlines Flight 3054]] overruns the runway of [[São Paulo–Congonhas Airport]] and crashes, killing all 187 and 12 others on the ground.
* [[July 21]] &ndash; The final book in [[J. K. Rowling]]'s [[Harry Potter]] series, ''[[Harry Potter and the Deathly Hallows]]'', is released and sells over 11 million copies in the first 24 hours, becoming the fastest selling book in history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iht.com/articles/2007/07/23/business/potter.php |title='Harry Potter' tale is fastest-selling book in history|work=[[International Herald Tribune]]|date=2007-07-23|accessdate=2011-05-21|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20110603233204/http://www.iht.com/articles/2007/07/23/business/potter.php|archivedate=3 June 2011<!--DASHBot-->|deadurl=no}}</ref>
* [[July 24]] Five [[Bulgaria]]n nurses were released from Libyan prison after eight and a half years spent behind the bars in [[Benghazi]] and [[Tripoli]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/6912965.stm|title=BBC NEWS - Europe - HIV medics released to Bulgaria|work=bbc.co.uk|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref> The release marked the end of the famous [[HIV trial in Libya]], known also as "Bulgarian nurses affair".

===August===
[[File:35wBridgecollapse.gif|thumb|alt=animated GIF of the bridge collapse|The collapse of [[35W Bridge]] in [[Minneapolis]]]]
* [[August 1]] &ndash; The [[35W Bridge]] in [[Minneapolis]], [[Minnesota]], collapses, resulting in 13 deaths.
* [[August 4]] &ndash; The [[Phoenix (spacecraft)|Phoenix spacecraft]] is launched toward the Martian north pole.
* [[August 6]] &ndash; [[Israel]]i Prime Minister [[Ehud Olmert]] arrives in the historic Palestinian town of [[Jericho]], becoming the first Prime Minister of Israel to visit the [[West Bank]] or [[Gaza Strip]] in more than seven years. Olmert meets with Palestinian Authority President [[Mahmoud Abbas]].
* [[August 9]] &ndash; The French global bank [[BNP Paribas]] in the United Kingdom blocks withdrawals from three [[hedge fund]]s heavily committed in [[sub-prime mortgage]]s, signaling the [[2007–2012 global financial crisis]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Three myths that sustain the economic crisis|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/business/economics-blog/2012/aug/05/economic-crisis-myths-sustain|accessdate=2012-08-06|newspaper=[[The Guardian]]|location=London|date=2012-08-05|first=Larry|last=Elliott}}</ref>
* [[August 14]] &ndash; Multiple suicide bombings [[2007 Yazidi communities bombings|kill 572 people]] in [[Qahtaniyah, Iraq|Qahtaniya]], northern [[Iraq]].
* [[August 15]] &ndash; An [[2007 Peru earthquake|8.0 earthquake]] strikes [[Peru]], killing 512 people, injuring more than 1,500, and causing [[tsunami]] warnings in the Pacific Ocean.
* [[August 17]] &ndash; [[Vladimir Putin]] issues a statement revealing that Russia is to resume the flight exercises of its [[strategic bomber]]s in remote areas. The flights were suspended in [[1991]] after the [[dissolution of the Soviet Union]].

===September===
* [[September 2]]&ndash;[[September 9]] &ndash; The [[Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation]] summit hosts its [[APEC Australia 2007|19th annual city meeting]] in Sydney.
* [[September 6]] &ndash; ''[[Operation Orchard]]'': Israeli airplanes strike a suspected nuclear site in Syria.
* [[September 14]] &ndash; The [[SELENE]] spacecraft launches. [[Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency|JAXA]] has called the mission, "the largest [[Moon|lunar]] mission since the [[Apollo program]]."
* [[September 16]] &ndash; [[One-Two-GO Airlines Flight 269]] crashes in [[Phuket (city)|Phuket]], [[Thailand]], killing 89 passengers and crew.
* [[September 20]] &ndash; The [[2007 Universal Forum of Cultures]] opens in [[Monterrey]], Mexico.
* [[September 24]]
** [[India national cricket team|India]] win the inaugural [[2007 ICC World Twenty20]] Cup, beating [[Pakistan national cricket team|Pakistan]] in the final.
** [[American Broadcasting Company|ABC]] launches a new on-air identity.

===October===
* [[October 4]] &ndash; Spanish authorities arrest 22 people associated with the banned [[Batasuna]] party, which campaigns for [[Basque Country (greater region)|Basque]] independence, but also has ties to the terrorist group [[ETA (separatist group)|ETA]].
* [[October 8]] &ndash; Track and field star [[Marion Jones]] surrenders the five [[Olympic Games|Olympic]] medals she won in the [[2000 Summer Olympics|2000 Sydney Games]], after admitting to [[doping in sport|doping]].
* [[October 14]] &ndash; Al-habileen/[[lahij]]: Four citizens are killed on the 44th anniversary of the revolution against [[British Empire|British]] colonial rule in [[South Yemen]].
* [[October 24]] &ndash; In the space of a few hours, [[Comet Holmes]] develops a coma and flares up to half a million times its former brightness, becoming visible to the naked eye. Its coma later becomes larger in volume than the [[Sun]], the second such comet in 2007 after Comet McNaught.
* [[October 28]] &ndash; The [[Holy See|Vatican]] [[beatification|beatifies]] [[498 Spanish Martyrs|498 Spanish victims]] of religious persecution from before and during the [[Spanish Civil War]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20071028/ap_on_re_eu/vatican_spain_beatifications|title=Vatican beatifies 498 Spanish martyrs |publisher=Yahoo News |accessdate=2007-10-28| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20071030054521/http://news.yahoo.com/s/ap/20071028/ap_on_re_eu/vatican_spain_beatifications| archivedate= 30 October 2007 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= yes}}</ref>
* [[October 31]] &ndash; The [[World Economic Forum]] releases [[Global Competitiveness Report|The Global Competitiveness Report 2007-2008]].

===November===
* [[November 3]] &ndash; President [[Pervez Musharraf]] declares a [[Pakistani state of emergency, 2007|state of emergency]] in [[Pakistan]].
* [[November 5]] &ndash; The [[2007–08 Writers Guild of America strike|Writers Guild of America goes on a strike]] that lasts until [[February 12]], [[2008]].
* [[November 6]] &ndash; A suicide bomber kills at least 50 people in [[Mazar-i-Sharif]], [[Afghanistan]], including 6 members of the [[National Assembly (Afghanistan)|National Assembly]].
* [[November 13]] &ndash; An [[Batasang Pambansa bombing|explosion]] hits the south wing of the [[House of Representatives of the Philippines]] in [[Quezon City]], north of [[Manila]], killing 4 people, including [[Basilan]] Congressman [[Wahab Akbar]], and wounding 6 others.
* [[November 14]]
** [[High Speed 1]] from London to the [[Channel Tunnel]] is opened to passengers.
** The 7.7 [[Moment magnitude scale|Mw]] [[2007 Tocopilla earthquake|Tocopilla earthquake]] shakes northern [[Chile]] with a maximum [[Mercalli intensity scale|Mercalli intensity]] of VIII (''Severe''), leaving two dead and sixty-five injured.
* [[November 16]] &ndash; Approximately 10,000 people are believed to have died after [[Cyclone Sidr]] hits [[Bangladesh]].
* [[November 18]] &ndash; The [[2007 Zasyadko mine disaster|Zasyadko mine disaster]] in eastern [[Ukraine]] claims the lives of 101 miners.
* [[November 30]] &ndash; [[Rambhadracharya]], a [[Hindu]] religious leader, released the first [[Braille]] version of [[Bhagavad Gita]], with the original [[Sanskrit]] text and a [[Hindi]] commentary at New Delhi.

===December===
* [[December 3]] &ndash; [[December 14|14]] &ndash; The [[United Nations Climate Change Conference]] is held at [[Nusa Dua]] in [[Bali]], [[Indonesia]].
* [[December 7]] &ndash; [[Uranus]]'s [[orbit]] is positioned such that the [[sun]] shines directly above its [[equator]] (i.e. an [[equinox]]).
* [[December 8]] &ndash; The [[2007 Africa–EU Summit]] takes place as [[European Union]] and [[African Union]] leaders gather in [[Lisbon]], Portugal, for their first joint summit in 7 years. The British and [[Czech Republic|Czech]] prime ministers boycott the event due to the presence of [[List of Presidents of Zimbabwe|Zimbabwean President]] [[Robert Mugabe]].
* [[December 10]] &ndash; The [[United Nations]] deadline for a negotiated settlement on the future of [[Kosovo]] passes without an international agreement.
* [[December 16]] &ndash; [[Ron Paul]] sets a record by raising over six million dollars online in just 24 hours, an event known as a [[moneybomb]].
* [[December 19]] &ndash; [[Vladimir Putin]], [[President of Russia]], is announced as [[Time (magazine)|Time magazine]]'s 2007 [[Time Person of the Year|Person of the Year]].
* [[December 20]] &ndash; The [[Pablo Picasso]] painting ''[[Portrait of Suzanne Bloch]]'', together with [[Candido Portinari]]'s ''O Lavrador de Café'', is stolen from the [[São Paulo Museum of Art]].
* [[December 21]] &ndash; The Czech Republic, [[Estonia]], Hungary, [[Latvia]], [[Lithuania]], [[Malta]], Poland, [[Slovakia]], and [[Slovenia]] join the [[Schengen Agreement|Schengen]] border-free zone.
* [[December 24]] &ndash; The [[Nepal]]ese government announces that the country's 240-year-old [[Kingdom of Nepal|monarchy]] will be abolished in [[2008]] and a new [[republic]] will be declared.
* [[December 27]]
** Former [[Pakistan]]i prime minister [[Benazir Bhutto]] is assassinated, and at least 20 others are [[Assassination of Benazir Bhutto|killed, by a bomb blast]] at an election rally in [[Rawalpindi]].
**Riots erupt in [[Mombasa]], [[Kenya]], after [[Mwai Kibaki]] is declared the winner of the [[Kenyan presidential election, 2007|presidential election]], triggering a [[2007–08 Kenyan crisis|political, economic, and humanitarian crisis]].
