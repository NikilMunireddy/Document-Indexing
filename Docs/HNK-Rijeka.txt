'''Hrvatski Nogometni Klub Rijeka''' ({{lang-en|Croatian Football Club Rijeka}}), commonly referred to as '''HNK Rijeka''' or simply '''Rijeka''', is a [[Croatia]]n [[association football|football]] club, from the coastal city of [[Rijeka]]. During the reconstruction of [[Stadion Kantrida]], their traditional home ground, Rijeka play their home matches at [[Stadion Rujevica]]. Rijeka's traditional home colours are all white.

The club was founded on 29 July 1946 as '''NK Kvarner''', and changed its name to '''NK Rijeka''' on 3 June 1954.<ref name="Povijest">{{cite web|url=http://nk-rijeka.hr/povijest|title=Povijest|author=HNK Rijeka|publisher=HNK Rijeka official website|language=Croatian|accessdate=22 February 2015}}</ref> Since its foundation, the club has won two [[Yugoslav Cup]] titles, three [[Croatian Football Cup]]s, one [[Croatian Football Super Cup]] and the 1978 [[Balkans Cup]]. The club competes in Croatia's top division, [[Prva HNL]], of which it has been a member since its foundation in 1992.

==History==

===Origins===
The club was founded as '''NK Kvarner''' in 1946, after [[Rijeka]] (known by its Italian name ''Fiume'' until 1945) was ceded from [[Italy]] to [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslavia]] following the end of [[World War II]]. Some dispute this and claim that the club's roots date back to 1926, the founding of [[U.S. Fiumana]], which competed at various levels of the [[Italian Football Championship]], including a season in the top tier competition (''Divisione Nazionale'') in 1928. Their claim is based on evidence that demonstrates a clear continuity between the two clubs. For example, besides using the same grounds ([[Stadion Kantrida]]), as many as eight U.S. Fiumana players later played for NK Kvarner.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rijekadanas.com/split-slavi-zagreb-izmislja-opatija-ne-zna-a-rijeka-se-srami/|title=Split slavi, Zagreb izmišlja, Opatija ne zna, a Rijeka se srami|publisher=RijekaDanas|language=Croatian|date=26 April 2011|accessdate=2 March 2012}}</ref>

===1946–1991===
During the early period in Yugoslavia, Kvarner had moderate success in various [[Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia|Yugoslav]] and local club championships. They were relegated at the end of their inaugural season in the [[Yugoslav First League]] in [[1946–47 Yugoslav First League|1946–47]]. Kvarner changed their name to '''NK Rijeka''' on 3 June 1954 and returned to the [[Yugoslav First League]] in 1958.<ref name="Povijest"/> Rijeka remained in the top tier for eleven consecutive seasons, until 1969, when they were once again relegated to the [[Yugoslav Second League]].<ref name="Povijest"/> Despite finishing on top in four (out of five) seasons in the second tier, due to three failed qualifying attempts, the club only gained promotion back to the top tier in 1974. With varying success, Rijeka remained in top tier until the [[breakup of Yugoslavia]] in 1991.<ref name="Povijest"/> The club's greatest success during this period involved back-to-back [[Yugoslav Cup]] titles in [[1977–78 Yugoslav Cup|1978]] and [[1978–79 Yugoslav Cup|1979]]. Rijeka were also a Cup runner-up in [[1986–87 Yugoslav Cup|1987]], when they lost the final after a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Puric |last2=Schöggl |last3=Stokkermans |title=Yugoslavia/Serbia and Montenegro&nbsp;– Cup Finals |date=8 May 2014 |publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]] |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesj/joegcuphist.html |accessdate=8 May 2014}}</ref> In the [[Yugoslav First League]], Rijeka never finished above the fourth place. In [[1983–84 Yugoslav First League|1984]], the club came closest to their first championship title, finishing only two points behind [[Red Star Belgrade|Red Star]]. Rijeka were also the best placed Croatian club in the [[Yugoslav First League]] in [[1964–65 Yugoslav First League|1965]], [[1983–84 Yugoslav First League|1984]] and [[1986–87 Yugoslav First League|1987]].<ref name="hocuri.com">{{cite web |last=Vivoda|first=Vlado|title=HNK Rijeka: The Rise of the Phoenix |date=16 July 2014 |publisher=Hocuri.com |language=English |url=http://www.hocuri.com/hnk-rijeka-rise-phoenix/ |accessdate=20 November 2015}}</ref>

===Rijeka in the HNL===
Following the breakup of Yugoslavia, in 1992, Rijeka joined the [[Croatian First League]] in its [[1992 Prva HNL|inaugural season]]. Rijeka remain one of only four clubs that have never been relegated. In terms of its greatest successes in this period, the club won three [[Croatian Football Cup|Croatian Cups]], including back-to-back titles in [[2004–05 Croatian Football Cup|2005]] and [[2005–06 Croatian Football Cup|2006]], and in [[2013–14 Croatian Football Cup|2014]]. Rijeka also finished as runner-up in [[1993–94 Croatian Football Cup|1994]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stokkermans |title=Croatia&nbsp;– Cup Finals |date=26 September 2014 |publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]] |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesk/kroacuphist.html |accessdate=26 September 2014}}</ref> In the [[Croatian First Football League|Prva HNL]], the club finished as runner-up on five occasions, in [[1998–99 Prva HNL|1999]], [[2005–06 Prva HNL|2006]], [[2013–14 Prva HNL|2014]], [[2014–15 Prva HNL|2015]] and [[2015–16 Croatian First Football League|2016]].<ref name="hocuri.com"/> In the final round of the [[1998–99 Prva HNL|1998–99]] season, a refereeing error denied Rijeka their first championship title. With one match to go, Rijeka were one point ahead of [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Croatia Zagreb]], needing a home win against [[NK Osijek|Osijek]] to secure the title. With the match tied at 1–1, in the 89th minute, Rijeka forward [[Admir Hasančić]] converted a cross by [[Barnabás Sztipánovics]]. The packed [[Stadion Kantrida|Kantrida]] erupted in celebration. However, to their dismay, moments later, assistant referee Krečak raised his flag and referee Šupraha disallowed Rijeka's winning goal for alleged offside.<ref>{{cite web |last1= |title=Rijeka-Osijek 1:1 |date=26 May 1999 |publisher=HRnogomet.com |language=Croatian |url=http://hrnogomet.com/hnl/utakmica.php?id=1875 |accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref> Following an investigation, [[Three-dimensional space|3D]] analysis revealed that Hasančić was not in an offside position (clear by 12 centimeters) and that Rijeka were wrongfully denied their first championship title.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Vidalina |title=Dokaz! Rijeci 1999. naslov prvaka definitivno ukraden |date=1 June 2009 |publisher=[[24sata (Croatia)|24 sata]] |language=Croatian |url=http://www.24sata.hr/nogomet/dokaz-rijeci-1999-naslov-prvaka-definitivno-ukraden-119510 |accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Bajruši |title=Dokumenti koji otkrivaju kako je Dinamo 1999. ukrao prvenstvo |date=13 August 2002 |publisher=[[Nacional (weekly)|Nacional]] |language=Croatian |url=http://arhiva.nacional.hr/clanak/10330/dokumenti-koji-otkrivaju-kako-je-dinamo-1999-ukrao-prvenstvo |accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref> An investigation by ''[[Nacional (weekly)|Nacional]]'' revealed that [[Franjo Tuđman]], the President of the Republic of Croatia and an avid [[GNK Dinamo Zagreb|Croatia Zagreb]] supporter, earlier in 1999 ordered the country's intelligence agencies to spy on football referees, officials and journalists, with the aim of ensuring that the Zagreb club wins the league title.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bajruši |title=Dokumenti koji otkrivaju kako je Dinamo 1999. ukrao prvenstvo |date=13 August 2002 |publisher=[[Nacional (weekly)|Nacional]] |language=Croatian |url=http://arhiva.nacional.hr/clanak/10330/dokumenti-koji-otkrivaju-kako-je-dinamo-1999-ukrao-prvenstvo |accessdate=30 January 2016}}</ref>

===European record===
Rijeka participated in [[UEFA]] competitions on 15 occasions. The greatest success was the quarter-final of the [[1979–80 European Cup Winners' Cup]], where they lost to Italian giants [[Juventus]] 2–0 on aggregate.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Stokkermans |title=Cup Winners' Cup 1979–80 |date=9 January 2008 |publisher=[[Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation]] |url=http://www.rsssf.com/ec/ec197980.html#cwc |accessdate=9 January 2008}}</ref> The most memorable result in Europe was the home win (3–1) against the eventual winners [[Real Madrid]] in the [[1984–85 UEFA Cup]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.armada-rijeka.hr/site/2012/10/24-10-1984-rijeka-real-madrid-31/|title=24.10.1984: Rijeka&nbsp;– Real Madrid 3:1|publisher=Armada Rijeka|language=Croatian|accessdate=24 October 2012}}</ref> Controversially, in the return leg at [[Santiago Bernabéu Stadium|Santiago Bernabéu]], which Rijeka lost 3–0, three of their players were sent off and the Belgian referee Roger Schoeters ensured that the eventual competition winner progressed to the next round. Madrid scored their first goal from a doubtful penalty in the 67th minute with Rijeka already down to ten men. Over the next ten minutes, two additional Rijeka players were sent off, most notably [[Damir Desnica]]. While Desnica received the first yellow card because he did not stop play after Schoeters blew his whistle; the second ensued because he, allegedly, insulted the referee. However, unbeknownst to the referee, Desnica had been a [[deaf-mute]] since birth.<ref name="hocuri.com"/> With Rijeka reduced to eight players, Madrid scored two additional goals, progressed to the next round and eventually won the trophy. In 2013, by winning 4–3 on aggregate against [[VfB Stuttgart|Stuttgart]] from Germany, Rijeka qualified for the Group Stages of the [[2013–14 UEFA Europa League]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novilist.hr/Sport/Nogomet/Velika-noc-na-Kantridi-Benko-Kvrzic-i-Vargic-za-povijest|title=Velika noć na Kantridi: Benko, Kvržić i Vargić za povijest|publisher=Novi list|language=Croatian|accessdate=22 August 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novilist.hr/Sport/Nogomet/Stuttgart-Rijeka-2-2-bijeli-u-Europskoj-ligi|title=Stuttgart&nbsp;– Rijeka 2:2, bijeli u Europskoj ligi|publisher=Novi list|language=Croatian|accessdate=29 August 2013}}</ref> Rijeka also participated in the Group Stages of the [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League]], where they defeated [[Feyenoord]] and [[Standard Liège]], and drew with title-holders and eventual winners [[Sevilla FC|Sevilla]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novilist.hr/Sport/Nogomet/Hat-trick-za-povijesnu-pobjedu-u-Europskoj-ligi-Kramaric-Feyenoord-3-1|title=Hat trick za povijesnu pobjedu u Europskoj ligi: Kramarić&nbsp;– Feyenoord 3:1|publisher=Novi list|language=Croatian|accessdate=23 October 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novilist.hr/Sport/Nogomet/Blago-nama-Moises-i-Krama-Bijeli-u-pola-sata-pomeli-Standard|title=Blago nama, Moises i Krama: Bijeli u pola sata pomeli Standard|publisher=Novi list|language=Croatian|accessdate=27 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.novilist.hr/Sport/Nogomet/Peh-u-sudackoj-nadoknadi-Sevilla-u-zadnji-cas-iscupala-bod-na-Kantridi|title=Peh u sudačkoj nadoknadi: Sevilla u posljednjim sekundama iščupala bod na Kantridi|publisher=Novi list|language=Croatian|accessdate=2 October 2014}}</ref>

===Private ownership===
In February 2012, [[Gabriele Volpi]], an Italian businessman, the founder of Orlean Invest, and also the owner of [[Spezia Calcio]] and [[Pro Recco]], injected much needed capital into the club. With the privatization process complete by September 2013, Volpi, through Dutch-based Stichting Social Sport Foundation, became the owner of 70 percent of the club, with the [[Rijeka|City of Rijeka]] in control of the remaining 30 percent.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.index.hr/sport/clanak/rijeka-postala-sportsko-dionicko-drustvo-volpi-dao-54-milijuna-kuna-za-70-posto-vlasnistva/703532.aspx|title=Rijeka postala sportsko dioničko društvo: Volpi dao 54 milijuna kuna za 70 posto vlasništva|publisher=[[Index.hr]]|language=Croatian|date=30 September 2013|accessdate=12 October 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nk-rijeka.hr/dobrodoslica-predsjednika/ownership/?lang=en|title=Ownership|publisher=nk-rijeka.hr|language=English|date=|accessdate=20 November 2015}}</ref>

===Record transfer===
In January 2015, Rijeka sold their star striker [[Andrej Kramarić]] to [[Leicester City F.C.|Leicester City]] for a club-record £9.7 million.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.espnfc.com.au/story/2232121/andrej-kramaric-set-to-complete-97m-move-to-leicester-from-rijekasubject-to-work-permit|title=Andrej Kramaric set to complete £9.7m move to Leicester from Rijeka|publisher=[[ESPN]]|date=8 January 2015|accessdate=26 April 2015}}</ref>

==Stadium==
{{main|Stadion Kantrida|Stadion Rujevica}}
Until July 2015, Rijeka were based at [[Stadion Kantrida]], their traditional home ground for over 60 years. With Kantrida awaiting demolition and reconstruction, since August 2015, Rijeka have been based at the newly-built [[Stadion Rujevica]], with a seating capacity of 6,000. Rujevica is part of the club's new training camp and serves as their temporary home ground. Following the demolition of old Kantrida, a new, state of the art, stadium will be built at the same location. The commencement of construction of the 14,600 all-seater is scheduled for 2017.<ref>[http://www.stadionkantrida.hr/en/ Official Project Website]</ref>

==Support and rivalries==
{{main|Armada Rijeka}}
Rijeka's hardcore supporters are the so-called ''[[Armada Rijeka]]'', or simply Armada. The [[ultras]] group has been active since 1987.

{{Further |Adriatic derby|Dinamo–Rijeka derby|Rijeka–Pula derby (Rijeka vs Istra 1961)}}
Rijeka's greatest rivalry is with [[Hajduk Split]]. Since 1946, the [[Adriatic derby]] is contested between the two most popular Croatian football clubs from the [[Adriatic Sea|Adriatic]] coast, Rijeka and [[HNK Hajduk Split|Hajduk]]. Other rivalries exist with [[Dinamo Zagreb]] and, on regional level, with [[NK Istra 1961|Istra Pula]]. The origins of the Rijeka-Pula rivalry date back to the clashes between [[U.S. Fiumana]] and [[G.S.F. Giovanni Grion Pola|G.S.F. Grion Pola]] since the late 1920s.

==Honours==
Rijeka won two [[Yugoslav Cup]] titles and three [[Croatian Football Cup|Croatian Cup]]s. In European competitions, the club has reached the quarter-final of the [[UEFA Cup Winners' Cup|Cup Winners' Cup]] in [[1979–80 European Cup Winners' Cup|1979–80]], and [[UEFA Europa League]] Group Stage in [[2013–14 UEFA Europa League|2013–14]] and [[2014–15 UEFA Europa League|2014–15]].
