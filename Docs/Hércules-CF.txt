'''Hércules de Alicante Club de Fútbol, S.A.D.''' ({{IPA-es|ˈerkules}}) is a [[Spanish football league teams|Spanish football team]] based in [[Alicante]], in the autonomous community of [[Valencian Community]]. Founded in 1922 it currently plays in [[Segunda División B]], and holds home games at the ''[[Estadio José Rico Pérez]]'', which seats 30,000 spectators.

==History==
After first appearing in [[La Liga]] in the [[1935–36 La Liga|1935–36 season]], Hércules would play sporadically in the category for the next forty years, playing mainly in the [[Segunda División|second division]] but going as low as [[Tercera División|the third]]. From 1961 to 1969, neighbours [[Alicante CF]] acted as its [[Farm team|feeder club]].

After a ten-year spell in the top flight, encompassing 12 seasons in the 1970s/80s, the club only returned again in the [[1996–97 La Liga|1996–97 campaign]]: though eventually relegated it managed two remarkable comeback wins over [[FC Barcelona]], which ultimately handed the league title to [[Real Madrid C.F.|Real Madrid]].

In [[2004–05 Segunda División B|2004–05]], after five years in the [[Segunda División B|third level]], Hércules finished second, being subsequently promoted to the "silver category". After posting three consecutive solid seasons, the club narrowly missed out on a return to the top division in [[2008–09 Segunda División|2009]], finishing fourth three points behind last-promotee [[CD Tenerife]].

[[File:Plantilla Hércules Ayuntamiento 2010.JPG|thumb|200px|left|Hércules celebrate in [[Alicante]], after returning to [[La Liga]] in 2010]] 
The [[2009–10 Segunda División|2009–10 campaign]] saw Hércules promoted back into the top flight after 13 years, in dramatic fashion: losing 0–1 at [[half-time]] at [[Rayo Vallecano]], the team fought back to win 2–1 in the penultimate game of the season and leap frog [[Real Betis]] into third place. In the last round, a 2–0 away win against relegation-threatened [[Real Unión]] guaranteed promotion, with the 4–0 win of Betis over [[Levante UD]] eventually counting for nothing (all three teams – Levante, Hércules and Betis – ended with the same number of points).
[[File:Alineación titular Hércules jornada1 temp.2010-11.jpg|thumb|300px|right|First starting lineup of the season 2010/11, concerning the match between Hércules and [[Athletic Bilbao]] played at the José Rico Pérez Stadium.]]
Early into the [[2010–11 La Liga|2010–11 season]], one year, three months and 19 days after Barcelona's last home defeat in the league, Hércules recorded a shock 2–0 win at the [[Camp Nou]], thanks to a brace from [[Nelson Haedo Valdez|Nelson Valdez]] –<ref>{{cite web|url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/report?id=301997&cc=5739&league=ESP.1|title=Valdez brace sinks champions|date=2010-09-11|publisher=[[ESPN Soccernet]]|date=11 September 2010|accessdate=29 September 2010}}</ref> ''Barça'' had won their last 11 home matches, scored at least three times in each of their last six league fixtures and were protecting a 17-game unbeaten streak. Amazingly, however, this was the Alicante outfit's third successive win over the [[Catalonia|Catalans]], having won both meetings in their previous top flight campaign [[1996–97 La Liga|14 years before]];<ref>[http://www.fifa.com/worldfootball/statisticsandrecords/news/newsid=1302115.html?cid=newsletter_en_20100916_newsid1302115 Remarkable runs and a seismic shock]; FIFA.com, 15 September 2010</ref> after a solid first round of 19 games the team slumped in the table, eventually ranking in 19th position for an immediate relegation back.

In [[2013-14 Segunda División|2013-14 campaign]] they were relegated, now to [[Segunda División B]], finishing in dead last.
