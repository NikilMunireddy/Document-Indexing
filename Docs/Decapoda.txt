The '''Decapoda''' or '''decapods''' (literally "ten-footed") are an order of [[crustacean]]s within the class [[Malacostraca]], including many familiar groups, such as [[crayfish]], [[crab]]s, [[lobster]]s, [[Dendrobranchiata|prawns]], and [[Caridea|shrimp]]. Most decapods are [[scavenger]]s. The order is estimated to contain nearly 15,000 species in around 2,700 genera, with around 3,300 fossil species.<ref name="Grave">{{cite journal |journal = [[Raffles Bulletin of Zoology]] |year=2009 |volume = Suppl. 21 |pages=1–109 |title = A classification of living and fossil genera of decapod crustaceans |author1=Sammy De Grave |author2=N. Dean Pentcheff |author3=Shane T. Ahyong |url=http://rmbr.nus.edu.sg/rbz/biblio/s21/s21rbz1-109.pdf|display-authors=etal}}</ref> Nearly half of these species are crabs, with the shrimp (about 3000 species) and [[Anomura]] (including [[hermit crab]]s, [[porcelain crab]]s, [[squat lobster]]s (about 2500 species) making up the bulk of the remainder.<ref name="Grave"/> The earliest [[fossil]] decapod is the [[Devonian]] ''[[Palaeopalaemon]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://palaeo.gly.bris.ac.uk/Palaeofiles/Fossilgroups/Decapoda/Fossilrecord.html |title=Order Decapoda: Fossil record and evolution |publisher=[[University of Bristol]] |author=Robert P. D. Crean |accessdate=January 2, 2010 |date=November 14, 2004}}</ref>

==Anatomy==
{{main|Decapod anatomy}}
As the name Decapoda (from the [[Greek language|Greek]] {{lang|el|[[:wikt:δέκα|δέκα]]}}, ''{{lang|el|[[:wikt:deca-|deca-]]}}'', "ten", and {{lang|el|[[:wikt:πούς|πούς]] / ποδός}}, ''[[:wikt:-pod|-pod]]'', "foot") implies, all decapods have ten legs, in the form of five pairs of thoracic appendages on the last five thoracic segments. The front three pairs function as [[Arthropod mouthparts|mouthparts]] and are generally referred to as [[maxilliped]]s; the remainder are [[pereiopod]]s. In many decapods, however, one pair of legs has enlarged pincers; the claws are called [[chela (organ)|chelae]], so those legs may be called chelipeds. Further appendages are found on the abdomen, with each segment capable of carrying a pair of biramous [[pleopod]]s, the last of which form part of the tail fan (together with the [[telson]]) and are called [[uropod]]s.

==Classification==
Classification within the order Decapoda depends on the structure of the [[gill]]s and legs, and the way in which the [[larva]]e develop, giving rise to two suborders: [[Dendrobranchiata]] and [[Pleocyemata]]. The Dendrobranchiata consist of prawns, including many species colloquially referred to as "shrimp", such as the "white shrimp", ''[[Litopenaeus setiferus]]''. The Pleocyemata include the remaining groups, including "true shrimp".<ref>{{cite book |author=Elena Mente |year=2008 |title=Reproductive Biology of Crustaceans: Case Studies of Decapod Crustaceans |publisher=[[Science Publishers]] |isbn=978-1-57808-529-3 |page=16}}</ref> Those groups which usually walk rather than swim (Pleocyemata, excluding Stenopodidea and Caridea) form a clade called Reptantia.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=G. Scholtz |author2=S. Richter |year=1995 |title=Phylogenetic systematics of the reptantian Decapoda (Crustacea, Malacostraca) |journal=[[Zoological Journal of the Linnean Society]] |volume=113 |pages=289–328 |doi=10.1006/zjls.1995.0011 |issue=3}}</ref>

This classification to the level of superfamilies follows De Grave ''et al.''<ref name="Grave"/>
