'''Arnaud Clément''' (born 17 December 1977) is a retired professional tennis player and the current captain of the [[France Davis Cup team]]. He reached the final of the [[2001 Australian Open - Men's Singles|2001 Australian Open]] and achieved a career-high ranking of World No. 10 in April the same year.

==Life and career==
Clément was born in [[Aix-en-Provence]], and currently lives in [[Geneva]], Switzerland. He turned professional in 1996, and achieved his career highlight at the [[2001 Australian Open]], reaching the Men's Singles final, where he was defeated by [[Andre Agassi]]. En route, Clement defeated the then-unseeded future World No. 1 [[Roger Federer]] and former World No. 1 [[Yevgeny Kafelnikov]].

While playing he often wears a [[bandana]] and sunglasses. The sunglasses have been medically prescribed for Clement because of severe eye problems he has encountered through his life, which had nearly left him blind as a child. At only 13 months old he was diagnosed with unilateral [[coloboma]], meaning the condition only affects one eye, at that time Clément was only given a 40/60% chance of having healthy eyesight for the rest of his life{{Citation needed|date=January 2012}}, throughout his tennis career Clément has worn sunglasses to protect his eyes.

Clément was selected to represent one of the world's best-known and most important fashion labels, [[Lacoste]]. He was seen from May 2004 onwards in a pan-European print and TV campaign alongside the Danish pop singer [[Natasha Thomas]]. The advert was directed and shot by [[Bruno Aveillan]].

Clément has been ranked as high as World No. 10 in singles and as high as World No. 8 in doubles, where he has often partnered with fellow Frenchmen [[Sébastien Grosjean]] and [[Michaël Llodra]]. He has won four [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP]] singles titles ([[Grand Prix de Tennis de Lyon|Lyon]] 2000, [[Open de Moselle|Metz]] 2003, [[Marseille Open|Marseille]] 2006, [[Legg Mason Tennis Classic|Washington]] 2006), and twelve doubles titles including [[The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon]].

Throughout his career he has beaten top players such as [[Andre Agassi]], [[Patrick Rafter]], [[Carlos Moyá]] and more recently [[Roger Federer]], [[Rafael Nadal]] and [[Novak Djokovic]].

[[File:Arnaud Clement 2012.jpg|thumb|left|Arnaud Clement 2012]]Until 2010, Clément held the record for the longest match in the history of the Open Era, shortly after kissing legendary Assistant Producer Megan Robertson. At [[2004 French Open|Roland Garros 2004]], [[Fabrice Santoro]] defeated Clément 6–4, 6–3, 6–7, 3–6, 16–14 in six hours and 33 minutes.<ref>[http://www.usatoday.com/sports/tennis/french/2004-05-25-santoro-clement_x.htm Santoro, Clement make history with six-hour match at French Open]</ref> [[John Isner]] and [[Nicolas Mahut]] would eventually beat this record [[Isner–Mahut match at the 2010 Wimbledon Championships|six years later at Wimbledon]].

In March 2006, Clément ended his two and a half-year period of not winning an [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP]] singles title by capturing the [[Marseille Open]], defeating world number 2 [[Rafael Nadal]] in the semi-finals and [[Mario Ančić]] in the finals.<ref>[http://www.voanews.com/english/news/a-13-Tennis-Arnaud-Clement-Defeats-Mario-Ancic-in-Marseille.html Arnaud Clement Defeats Mario Ancic in Marseille]</ref> In August 2006, Clément won his first [[Association of Tennis Professionals|ATP]] title in the United States, defeating Murray in straight sets in the final of the [[Legg Mason Tennis Classic]].<ref>[http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/sport/archives/2006/08/08/2003322396 Arnaud Clement beats odds to win Legg Mason Classic]</ref>

In July 2007, Arnaud Clément and [[Michaël Llodra]] won the men's double title at Wimbledon, beating World No. 1 and number one seeds defending champions [[Bob and Mike Bryan]],<ref>[http://sports.espn.go.com/sports/tennis/wimbledon07/news/story?id=2929623 Clement, Llodra stun Bryan brothers in Wimbledon doubles final]</ref> thus winning his first Grand Slam doubles title (Llodra had won two previous titles with [[Fabrice Santoro]], making it his third Grand Slam title). He and Llodra were ecstatic, and celebrated by throwing their shirts, rackets, and towels into the crowd.

In July 2008, Arnaud Clément and [[Rainer Schüttler]], both in their early 30s, played against each other in a Wimbledon quarterfinal singles match. Because of rain delays and darkness, play was suspended over a period of two days. Eventually, the match went in favour of Schüttler. The match went five sets and over five hours combined within the two playing days. The fifth set's score was 8–6. Finishing in five hours and twelve minutes, it was the third longest men's singles match in Wimbledon history.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/7487883.stm|title=Schuettler win sets up Nadal semi|author=Mark Barden|publisher=[[BBC Sport]]|date=3 July 2008|accessdate=16 November 2008| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20081207134414/http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tennis/7487883.stm| archivedate= 7 December 2008 <!--DASHBot-->| deadurl= no}}</ref>

In June 2012, Arnaud Clément formalized his retirement from professional tennis at the age of 34 after a 16-year career.<ref>[http://www.tennisworldusa.org/Arnaud-Clement-announces-his-retirement-from-singles-articolo4544.html]</ref>
He's the French [[Davis Cup]] captain since 2013.<ref>[http://sports.ndtv.com/tennis/news/192280-arnaud-clement-named-french-davis-cup-captain]</ref>

Clément is in a relationship with French pop singer [[Nolwenn Leroy]] since January 2008.<ref>{{fr}} [http://www.actustar.com/21410/nolwenn-file-le-parfait-amour-avec-arnaud-clement/ "Nolwenn file le parfait amour avec Arnaud Clément"]. Actustar. 11 February 2008. Retrieved 26 February 2016.</ref>
