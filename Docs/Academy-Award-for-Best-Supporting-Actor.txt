The '''Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor''' is an award presented annually by the [[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS). It is given in honor of an actor who has delivered an outstanding performance in a [[supporting actor|supporting]] role while working within the [[film industry]].

At the [[9th Academy Awards]] ceremony held in 1937, [[Walter Brennan]] was the first winner of this award for his role in ''[[Come and Get It (film)|Come and Get It]]''.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf|page=1|format=PDF|title=Rule One: Award Definitions | accessdate=August 30, 2013|publisher=[[Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences]] (AMPAS)}}</ref> Initially, winners in both supporting acting categories were awarded plaques instead of statuettes.<ref>{{harvnb|Kinn|Piazza|2014|p=39}}</ref> Beginning with the [[16th Academy Awards|16th ceremony]] held in 1944, however, winners received full-sized statuettes.<ref>{{harvnb|Kinn|Piazza|2014|p=67}}</ref> Currently, nominees are determined by [[single transferable vote]] within the actors branch of AMPAS; winners are selected by a [[Plurality (voting)|plurality vote]] from the entire eligible voting members of the Academy.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.oscars.org/sites/default/files/87aa_rules.pdf|page=8-7|format=PDF|title=Rule Six: Special Rules for the Acting Awards | accessdate=August 30, 2013|publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences (AMPAS)}}</ref>

Since its inception, the award has been given to 72 actors. Brennan has received the most awards in this category with three awards. Brennan, [[Robert Duvall]], [[Arthur Kennedy (actor)|Arthur Kennedy]], [[Jack Nicholson]], and [[Claude Rains]] were nominated on four occasions, more than any other actor. As of the [[87th Academy Awards|2016 ceremony]], [[Mark Rylance]] is the most recent winner in this category for his role as [[Rudolf Abel]] in ''[[Bridge of Spies (film)|Bridge of Spies]]''.
