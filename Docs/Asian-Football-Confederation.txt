The '''Asian Football Confederation''' ('''AFC''') is the governing body of [[association football]] in [[Asia]] and [[Australia]]. It has 47 member countries, mostly located on the Asian and Australian continent but excluding all the [[List of transcontinental countries#Asia and Europe|transcontinental countries]] with territory in both Europe and Asia, which are members of [[UEFA]] ([[Association of Football Federations of Azerbaijan|Azerbaijan]], [[Football Federation of Armenia|Armenia]], [[Georgian Football Federation|Georgia]], [[Cypriot Football Federation|Cyprus]], [[Football Federation of Kazakhstan|Kazakhstan]], [[Russian Football Union|Russia]], and [[Turkish Football Federation|Turkey]]). [[Israel Football Association|Israel]] is also a UEFA member even though it lies entirely in Asia. On the other hand, [[Football Federation Australia|Australia]], formerly in the [[Oceania Football Confederation|OFC]], joined the Asian Football Confederation [[Football Federation Australia#Move to AFC|in 2006]], and the [[Oceania]]n island of [[Guam Football Association|Guam]], a territory of the [[United States]], is also a member of AFC, in addition to [[Northern Mariana Islands Football Association|Northern Mariana Islands]], one of the [[Commonwealth (U.S. insular area)|Two Commonwealths]] of the [[United States]]. [[Hong Kong Football Association|Hong Kong]] and [[Macau Football Association|Macau]], although not independent countries (both are [[Special Administrative Region|Special administrative regions]] of [[China]]), are also members of the AFC.

One of [[FIFA]]'s six continental confederations, the AFC was formed officially on 8 May 1954 in [[Manila]], [[Philippines]], on the sidelines of the second [[1954 Asian Games|Asian Games]]. The main headquarters is located in [[Bukit Jalil]], [[Kuala Lumpur]], [[Malaysia]]. The current president is [[Salman Bin Ibrahim Al-Khalifa]] of [[Bahrain]].

==History==
The Asian Football Confederation was founded on 8 May 1954. [[Afghanistan Football Federation|Afghanistan]], [[Myanmar Football Federation|Burma (Myanmar)]], [[Chinese Taipei Football Association|Republic of China (Chinese Taipei)]], [[Hong Kong Football Association|Hong Kong]], [[All India Football Federation|India]], [[Israel Football Association|Israel]], [[Football Association of Indonesia|Indonesia]], [[Japan Football Association|Japan]], [[Korea Football Association|South Korea]], [[Pakistan Football Federation|Pakistan]], [[Philippine Football Federation|Philippines]], [[Football Association of Singapore|Singapore]] and [[Vietnam Football Federation|South Vietnam]] were founding members.<ref>香港足球總會九十週年紀念特刊 (Hong Kong Football Association 90th Anniversary Booklet) 2004</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.the-afc.com/60th-anniversary/afc-60th-anniversary-back-to-where-it-all-began |title=AFC 60th Anniversary: Back to where it all began |publisher=the-afc.com |date= |accessdate= }}</ref>

The Asian Ladies Football Confederation (ALFC) is the section of the AFC who manage [[women's football (soccer)|women's football]] in Asia.  The group was independently founded in April 1968 in a meeting involving [[Taiwan]], [[Hong Kong]], [[Malaysia]] and [[Singapore]]. In 1986 the ALFC merged with the AFC.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.asiansportsnet.com/football-soccer/|title=Football (Soccer) − Asian Football Confederation|publisher=Asian Sports Net|accessdate=25 March 2016}}</ref> The Asian Ladies Football Confederation helped organise the [[AFC Women's Asian Cup]], first held in 1975, as well as the AFC's [[AFC U-19 Women's Championship]] and the [[AFC U-17 Women's Championship]].

==Competitions==

===International===
The AFC runs the [[AFC Asian Cup]], [[AFC International Cup]] (official name to be announced) and [[AFC Women's Asian Cup]] - both competitions are held every four years and determine the Champions of Asia. The AFC also organises the [[AFC Futsal Championship]], [[AFC Beach Soccer Championship]], various age-level international youth football tournaments and the Asian qualifying tournament for the [[FIFA World Cup]], [[FIFA Women's World Cup]] and for [[football at the Summer Olympics]].

In addition to the AFC run international tournaments, each AFC regional federation organises its own tournament for national teams: [[EAFF East Asian Cup]], [[SAFF Championship]], [[AFF Championship]] and [[WAFF Championship]].

===Club===
The top-ranked AFC competition is the [[AFC Champions League]], which started in the [[2002–03 AFC Champions League|2002–03 season]] (an amalgamation of the [[Asian Champions Cup]] and the [[Asian Cup Winners Cup]]) and gathers the top 1–4 teams of each country (the number of teams depend on that country's ranking and can be upgraded or downgraded); this competition only gathered teams from top country.

A second, lower-ranked competition is the [[AFC Cup]]. This competition was launched by AFC in 2004. A third competition, the [[AFC President's Cup]], which had started in 2005, was absorbed into the AFC Cup in 2015.<ref>{{cite web |last=Griffiths |first=Ian |url=http://www.footballasia.com/en/news/index.asp?id=40247&sec=90&ssec=260&mth=7&yr=2005 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20051119163847/http://www.footballasia.com/en/news/index.asp?id=40247&sec=90&ssec=260&mth=7&yr=2005 |archivedate=2005-11-19 |title=Clubs sandwiched |work=FootballAsia.com |accessdate=2005-07-19}}</ref>

The AFC also runs an annual Asian [[futsal]] club competition, the [[AFC Futsal Club Championship]].
