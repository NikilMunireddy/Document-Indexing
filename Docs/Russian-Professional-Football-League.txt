The '''Professional Football League''' ({{lang-ru|Первенство Профессиональной футбольной лиги}}), formerly the '''Russian Second Division''' is the third level of Russian professional [[football (soccer)|football]].

In 1998–2010, it was run by the [[:ru:Профессиональная футбольная лига (Россия)|Professional Football League]]. The 2011–12 season was run by the Department of Professional Football of the [[Russian Football Union]] ({{lang-ru|Департамент профессионального футбола Российского футбольного союза (ДПФ РФС), Departament professional'nogo futbola Rossijskogo futbol'nogo soyuza (DPF RFS)}}).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://rfs.ru/main/news/ct148/73131.html |script-title=ru:Второй дивизион - очень важный пласт |date=6 December 2011 |publisher=Russian Football Union |accessdate=8 January 2012 |language=Russian}}</ref> From 2013–14 season the league is again run by the Professional Football League and the name Second Division is no longer used.

The PFL is geographically divided into 5 zones: ''West'' (Northwestern [[European Russia]]), ''Centre'' (Northern and Eastern European Russia), ''South'' (Southern European Russia), ''Ural-Povolzhye'' (Southern [[Ural Mountains|Urals]] and Western [[Siberia]]), and ''East'' (Rest of Siberia). The number of clubs in each zone varies between years. In the [[2015–16 Russian Professional Football League|2015–16 season]], there are 62 clubs in the division.

The winners of each zone are automatically promoted to the [[Russian Football National League|Russian National Football League]] (known before 2011 as the First Division). The bottom finishers of each zone lose professional status and are relegated to the [[Russian Amateur Football League]]. In the lowest populated ''East'' zone, each club plays each opponent three times, while in all other zones traditional home-and-away scheme applies.
