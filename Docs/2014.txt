'''2014''' ('''[[Roman numerals|MMXIV]]''') is a [[common year starting on Wednesday]] of the [[Gregorian calendar]]. It was the 2014th year of the [[Anno Domini]], or of [[Common Era]]; the 14th year of the [[3rd millennium]] and of the [[21st century]]; and the 5th of the [[2010s]].

Alternatively, the following rendition of the preceding lines adheres more strictly to [[WP:RY]] and avoids the [[category mistake]] of phrases like "Wednesday of the Gregorian calendar": '''2014''' ('''[[Roman numerals|MMXIV]]''') is a [[common year]] that [[Common year starting on Wednesday|started on a Wednesday]] and the current year. In the [[Gregorian calendar]], it is the 2014th year of the [[Common Era]] (CE) and ''[[Anno Domini]]'' (AD) designations; the 14th year of the [[3rd millennium]] and of the [[21st century]]; and the 5th of the [[2010s]] decade.-->

==Events==
===January===
* [[January 1]] &ndash; [[Latvia]] officially adopts the [[euro]] as its currency and becomes the 18th member of the [[Eurozone]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Latvia becomes 18th state to join the eurozone|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-25567096|accessdate=January 31, 2014|publisher=BBC News|date=January 1, 2014}}</ref>

===February===
* [[February]] &ndash; The [[Ebola virus epidemic in West Africa]] begins, infecting over 28,000 people and killing at least 11,000 people, the most severe both in terms of numbers of infections and casualties.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2014/07/03/health/ebola-outbreak-west-africa/ |title=Ebola virus: Can nations stop deadliest ever outbreak from spreading? |date=2014-03-07 |accessdate=2014-07-07}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web | url = http://www.cdc.gov/vhf/ebola/resources/outbreak-table.html | title = Chronology of Ebola Hemorrhagic Fever Outbreaks | publisher= Centers for Disease Control and Prevention | date = 24 June 2014 | accessdate = 25 June 2014}}</ref>
* [[February 7]]&ndash;[[February 23|23]] &ndash; The [[2014 Winter Olympics|XXII Olympic Winter Games]] are held in [[Sochi]], Russia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sochi2014.com/en|title=Sochi 2014 Winter Olympics - Olympic Videos, Photos, News|work=sochi2014.com|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbcolympics.com/medals|title=2012 Medal Standings|work=nbcolympics.com|accessdate=November 25, 2015}}</ref>
* [[February 13]] &ndash; Belgium becomes the first country in the world to legalise [[euthanasia]] for terminally ill patients of any age.<ref>{{cite news|date=February 13, 2014|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-26181615|title=Belgium's parliament votes through child euthanasia|publisher=BBC News|accessdate=February 13, 2014}}</ref>
* [[February 22]] &ndash; The [[Verkhovna Rada|Ukrainian parliament]] votes to remove [[President of Ukraine|President]] [[Viktor Yanukovych]] from office, replacing him with [[Oleksandr Turchynov]], after days of [[2014 Ukrainian revolution|civil unrest]] left around 100 people dead in [[Kiev]], [[Ukraine]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Ukraine: Speaker Oleksandr Turchynov named interim president|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-26312008|accessdate=February 23, 2014|publisher=BBC News|date=February 23, 2014}}</ref>
* [[February 26]] &ndash; The [[2014 pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine|pro-Russian unrest in Ukraine]] leads to the [[Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation|annexation of Crimea]] by the Russian Federation and an [[2014 insurgency in Donbass|insurgency]] in the Donetsk and Luhansk oblasts.

===March===
* [[March 5]] &ndash; [[Nicolás Maduro]], the [[List of Presidents of Venezuela|President of Venezuela]], severs diplomatic and political ties with [[Panama]], accusing Panama of being involved in a conspiracy against the [[Government of Venezuela|Venezuelan government]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Shoichet|first=Catherine E.|title=Venezuela cuts ties with Panama, calling country a 'lackey' for the United States|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2014/03/05/world/americas/venezuela-panama-diplomatic-ties-suspended/|accessdate=March 6, 2014|publisher=CNN|date=March 6, 2014}}</ref>
* [[March 8]] &ndash; [[Malaysia Airlines Flight 370]], a [[Boeing 777]] airliner en route to Beijing from [[Kuala Lumpur]], disappears over the [[Gulf of Thailand]] with 239 people on board. The aircraft is presumed to have crashed into the Indian Ocean.<ref>{{cite news|title=Search intensifies for Malaysian airliner and 239 people, rescue ships head to sea|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2014/03/07/world/asia/malaysia-airlines-plane-missing|accessdate=March 8, 2014|publisher=CNN|date=March 8, 2014|author=Watkins, Tom|author2=Carter, Chelsea J.}}</ref>
* [[March 16]] &ndash; A [[Crimean status referendum, 2014|referendum]] on the status of [[Crimea]] is held.<ref>{{cite news|title=Ukraine crisis: EU ponders Russia sanctions over Crimea vote|url=http://www.bbc.com/news/world-europe-26607466|accessdate=March 17, 2014|publisher=BBC News|date=March 17, 2014}}</ref>
* [[March 21]] &ndash; Russia formally [[2014 Crimean crisis|annexes Crimea]] after [[President of Russia|President]] [[Vladimir Putin]] signed a bill finalizing the annexation process.<ref>{{cite news|title=Russia officially annexes Crimea away from Ukraine with signature from Vladimir Putin|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/russia-annexes-crimea-away-from-ukraine-with-signature-from-vladimir-putin/|accessdate=March 23, 2014|publisher=AP via CBS News|date=March 21, 2014}}</ref>
* [[March 24]] &ndash; During an emergency meeting, the United Kingdom, the United States, Italy, Germany, France, Japan, and Canada temporarily suspend Russia from the [[G8]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Acosta|first=Jim|title=U.S., other powers kick Russia out of G8|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2014/03/24/politics/obama-europe-trip/|accessdate=March 25, 2014|publisher=CNN|date=March 25, 2014}}</ref>
* [[March 27]] &ndash; The [[United Nations General Assembly]] passes [[United Nations General Assembly Resolution 68/262|Resolution 68/262]], recognizing [[Crimea]] within Ukraine’s international borders and rejecting the validity of the [[2014 Crimean referendum]].<ref>{{cite web | url =http://www.un.org/en/ga/search/view_doc.asp?symbol=A/RES/68/262| title =Resolution adopted by the General Assembly on 27 March 2014  |publisher =[[United Nations]]| date=1 April 2014|accessdate =8 April 2014}}</ref>
* [[March 31]] &ndash; The [[United Nations]] [[International Court of Justice]] rules that Japan's [[Antarctic]] [[Whaling in Japan|whaling]] program is not scientific but commercial and forbids grants of further permits.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.com/news/world-asia-26818863#|title=(BBC)|work=BBC News|accessdate=October 6, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2014-03-31/ijc-japan-whaling-southern-ocean-scientific-research/5357416|title=ABC News Australia|work=ABC News|accessdate=October 6, 2014}}</ref>

* [[April 14]] &ndash; An estimated 276 girls and women are [[Chibok schoolgirls kidnapping|abducted and held hostage]] from a school in [[Nigeria]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/05/03/world/africa/nigeria-police-revise-number-of-kidnapped-students.html |title=Nigeria: Police Revise Number of Kidnapped Students |publisher=The New York Times |author=Associated Press |date=2014-02-05 |accessdate=2014-05-05}}</ref>
* [[April 16]] &ndash; Korean ferry [[Sinking of the MV Sewol|MV ''Sewol'' capsizes and sinks]] after an unmanageable cargo shift, killing 304 people, mostly high school students.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2014/07/18/world/asia/copter-crashes-after-searching-for-ferry-victims-in-korea.html?_r=0 |title=Copter Crashes After Search for Korean Ferry Victims |publisher=The New York Times |author=CHOE SANG-HUN |date=2014-07-17 |accessdate=2014-07-22}}</ref>
* [[April 27]] &ndash; The [[Catholic Church]] simultaneously [[canonization|canonizes]] Popes [[John XXIII]] and [[John Paul II]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Sainthood for John Paul II and John XXIII, as crowds pack St. Peter's Square|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2014/04/27/world/pope-canonization/?hpt=hp_t2|accessdate=April 27, 2014|publisher=CNN|date=April 27, 2014|author=Smith-Spark, Laura|author2=Gallagher, Delia|author3=Wedeman, Ben}}</ref>
* [[April 28]] &ndash; United States [[President of the United States|President]] [[Barack Obama]]'s new [[economic sanctions]] [[Russia–United States relations|against Russia]] go into effect, targeting companies and individuals close to [[President of Russia|Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]].<ref>{{cite news |last=Klimas |first=Jacqueline |title=New round of sanctions aimed at Putin, Russian elite |url=http://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2014/apr/27/us-placing-new-round-sanctions-russia/ |date=April 27, 2014 |work=[[Washington Post]] |accessdate=April 28, 2014}}</ref>

===July===
* [[July 8]]&ndash;[[August 26]] &ndash; Amid growing tensions between [[Israel]] and [[Hamas]] following the [[2014 kidnapping and murder of Israeli teenagers|kidnapping and murder]] of three Israeli teenagers in June and the [[Kidnapping and murder of Mohammed Abu Khdeir|revenge killing of a Palestinian teenager]] in July, Israel launches [[Operation Protective Edge]] on the [[State of Palestine|Palestinian]] [[Gaza Strip]] starting with numerous missile strikes, followed by a ground invasion a week later. In 7 weeks of fighting, 2,100 Palestinians and 71 Israelis are killed.
