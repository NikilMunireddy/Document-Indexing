Independent Albums

{{about|the Billboard record chart|information on albums which have been released independently|independent music}}
{{primary sources|date=January 2013}}
The '''Independent Albums''' chart (previously titled '''Top Independent Albums''') ranks the highest-selling [[independent music]] [[album]]s and [[extended play]]s (EPs) in the United States, as compiled by [[Nielsen SoundScan]] and published weekly by ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' magazine.<ref name="current chart">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/independent-albums#/charts/independent-albums|title=Independent Albums|publisher=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''|accessdate=2010-02-11}}</ref> It is used to list artists who are not signed to [[Record label#Major labels|major labels]]. Rankings are compiled by point-of-purchase sales obtained by Nielsen, and from legal [[music download]]s from a variety of [[online music store]]s. The chart began in the week of December 2, 2000.<ref name="billboard 20100211">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2000-12-02/independent-albums|title= Week of December 02, 2000 - Independent Albums|date=2000-12-02|publisher=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''|accessdate=2010-02-11}}</ref>

The top 25 positions are currently published through the ''Billboard'' website, with further chart positions available through a [[Subscription business model|paid subscription]] to Billboard.biz.<ref name="current chart"/> As with all ''Billboard'' charts, albums appearing on the Independent chart may also concurrently appear on the [[Billboard 200|''Billboard'' 200]], the main chart published based solely on sales, as well as any of the other ''Billboard'' chart. In addition, exclusive album titles which are only sold through individual retail sites may also be included in the chart, following a revised chart policy announced on November 7, 2007.<ref name="revision">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1047391/revised-chart-policy-lands-eagles-at-no-1|title=Revised Chart Policy Lands Eagles At No. 1|date=2007-11-07|publisher=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''|accessdate=2010-02-11}}</ref>

The chart's first number one was ''[[Who Let the Dogs Out (album)|Who Let the Dogs Out]]'' by [[Baha Men]],<ref name="billboard 20100211"/> which went on to top the year-end chart for 2001.<ref name="baha billboard">{{cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/77114/baha-men-move-to-follow-dogs|title=Baha Men 'Move' To Follow 'Dogs'|publisher=''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]''|accessdate=2010-01-26}}</ref>

==Best-selling top independent albums by year==
Since 2002, Billboard.biz has annually published an end-of-year list of the top fifty best selling independent albums. ''Billboard'' also independently announced the highest selling album for 2001. [[Lil Jon|Lil Jon & the East Side Boyz]] have topped this chart three times since its inception, twice with their 2002 album ''[[Kings of Crunk]]''.
